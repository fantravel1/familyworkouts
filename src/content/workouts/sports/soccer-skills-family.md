## Why Soccer is Perfect for Families

- **Minimal equipment**: Just a ball and open space
- **All skill levels**: Easy to start, lifetime to master
- **Great exercise**: Running, agility, coordination
- **Teamwork**: Everyone plays together

## What You'll Need

- Soccer ball (size 3 for young kids, size 4 for kids, size 5 for teens/adults)
- Cones or markers (shoes, water bottles work!)
- Open space (backyard, park, driveway)
- Comfortable shoes (cleats optional)

## Warm-Up: Ball Skills Intro (5 minutes)

### Toe Touches
1. Stand over the ball
2. Alternate tapping top of ball with bottom of feet
3. Right tap, left tap, right tap, left tap
4. Start slow, build speed
5. 1 minute

*"Get comfortable with the ball at your feet!"*

### Ball Rolls
1. Place foot on top of ball
2. Roll ball side to side under foot
3. 30 seconds each foot

### Pull Backs
1. Step on ball with one foot
2. Pull it backward
3. Stop it with other foot
4. 10 each foot

### Side-to-Side Rolls
1. Roll ball from right foot to left foot
2. Inside of right foot pushes to inside of left foot
3. Continuous motion
4. 30 seconds

## Station 1: Dribbling Skills (8 minutes)

### Basic Dribble

**Technique:**
- Use inside of both feet
- Keep ball close to you
- Light touches
- Look up, not always at the ball

**Drill: Cone Weave**
1. Set up 5-8 cones in a line, 3 feet apart
2. Dribble through, weaving around each cone
3. Inside of foot to push around cone
4. Both directions!
5. 2 minutes each direction

### Speed Dribble

**Technique:**
- Push ball ahead further
- Run to catch up
- Use top of foot (laces)
- For open space sprints

**Drill: Dribble Race**
1. Set up start and finish line (20-30 feet)
2. Race while dribbling
3. Ball must stay closeâ€”no kicking ahead!
4. Family races!
5. Best of 3

### Shielding

**Technique:**
- Body between opponent and ball
- Arm out for balance (not pushing!)
- Keep ball on far foot

**Drill: Shielding Challenge**
1. One person dribbles
2. Other person tries to steal (gently!)
3. Dribbler shields and protects
4. 30-second rounds, switch roles

## Station 2: Passing (8 minutes)

### Inside Foot Pass (Push Pass)

**Technique:**
1. Plant foot next to ball, pointing at target
2. Turn passing foot sideways
3. Strike ball with inside of foot
4. Follow through toward target
5. "Lock that ankle!"

**Drill: Partner Passing**
1. Partners 10-15 feet apart
2. Pass back and forth
3. Receive, control, pass
4. 20 passes each
5. Move further apart as skill improves

### Wall Pass (Give and Go)

**Technique:**
1. Pass to partner
2. Run past defender
3. Receive return pass
4. Simulated with 3 people!

**Drill: Triangle Passing**
1. Three family members form triangle
2. Pass around the triangle
3. Call the person's name before passing!
4. Both directions
5. Add movement: Follow your pass

### One-Touch Passing

**Technique:**
- Don't stop the ball
- Redirect it first time
- Faster play, requires anticipation

**Drill: One-Touch Circle**
1. Family in circle
2. Ball passed aroundâ€”no stopping!
3. How many passes before a miss?
4. Beat your record!

## Station 3: Shooting (8 minutes)

### Basic Shot

**Technique:**
1. Plant foot next to ball
2. Strike with laces (top of foot)
3. Toe down, ankle locked
4. Lean over the ball (not back!)
5. Follow through toward target

**Drill: Target Practice**
1. Set up "goals" (cones, shoes, bags)
2. Shoot from different distances
3. Start close, move back
4. 10 shots each, count successful goals

### Power Shot

**Technique:**
- Approach at slight angle
- Bigger backswing
- Strike through center of ball
- Full follow-through

**Drill: Shooting Competition**
1. Each person gets 5 shots
2. 1 point: On target
3. 2 points: In the goal
4. 3 points: Upper corners!
5. Total your family score

### Side Foot Finish (Placement)

**Technique:**
- Inside foot for accuracy
- Pick your spot
- Keeper can't save what they can't reach!

**Drill: Pick Your Corner**
1. Call which corner you're aiming for
2. Shoot for that corner
3. Accuracy over power
4. Switch corners each shot

## Station 4: Fun Games (6 minutes)

### Sharks and Minnows

**Setup:**
- Mark off a rectangle area
- One person is the "shark" in middle (no ball)
- Everyone else is "minnows" with balls

**Play:**
1. Minnows try to dribble across without shark stealing
2. If ball is stolen, minnow becomes shark too
3. Last minnow standing wins!

### Knockout

**Setup:**
- Everyone in a defined square area
- Everyone has a ball

**Play:**
1. Dribble your ball AND try to kick others' balls out
2. If your ball goes out, you're out
3. Last one with ball wins!

### World Cup

**Setup:**
- Small goals on each end
- Family divided into teams (or 1v1)

**Play:**
1. Small-sided game
2. First to 3 goals wins!
3. Everyone plays every position

## Cool-Down: Juggling Intro & Stretch (5 minutes)

### Juggling Practice

**Start Here:**
1. Hold ball, drop it
2. Kick up before second bounce
3. Catch it
4. Repeat 10 times each foot

**Progress To:**
- Two touches before catching
- Three touches
- Eventually: No hands!

*Juggling takes practiceâ€”celebrate small wins!*

### Stretches

**Quad Stretch** (30 sec each)
- Hold foot behind, balance

**Hamstring Stretch** (30 sec each)
- Seated or standing forward fold

**Hip Flexor** (30 sec each)
- Lunge position, stretch

**Calf Stretch** (30 sec each)
- Wall stretch, heel down

## Age Adaptations

### Preschool (3-5)
- Size 3 ball
- Focus on chasing and kicking
- Simple goals very close
- Let them score A LOT
- "Kick the ball to mommy/daddy!"

### Kids (6-12)
- Size 4 ball
- Can learn proper technique
- Love competitions and games
- Position awareness developing
- Consider local recreational league

### Teens
- Size 5 ball (regulation)
- Full techniques
- More complex drills
- Tactical awareness
- May teach parents a thing or two!

### Adults
- Great cardio workout
- Focus on technique over power
- Play keeper for kids' shots
- Join adult recreational leagues!

## Soccer Skills Progression

### Level 1: Beginner
- [ ] Can dribble forward 20 feet
- [ ] Can pass to a standing target
- [ ] Can shoot on an open goal
- [ ] Understands basic rules

### Level 2: Developing
- [ ] Can dribble through cones
- [ ] Can receive and pass in motion
- [ ] Can shoot with power AND accuracy
- [ ] Can play simple games

### Level 3: Confident
- [ ] Can dribble with both feet
- [ ] Can do give-and-go passing
- [ ] Can juggle 10+ times
- [ ] Can play positions effectively

### Level 4: Skilled
- [ ] Can dribble under pressure
- [ ] Can pass on the run
- [ ] Can juggle 50+ times
- [ ] Can make tactical decisions
