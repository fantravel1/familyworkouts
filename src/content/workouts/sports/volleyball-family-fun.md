# Volleyball Skills for the Whole Family

Volleyball is the ultimate family sport‚Äîcooperative, active, and tons of fun! Whether at the beach, park, or backyard, these skills translate anywhere.

## Why Volleyball for Families?

- **Team sport basics**: Cooperation and communication
- **All skill levels play together**: Rotate positions
- **Full-body workout**: Jumping, diving, moving
- **Beach or gym**: Adaptable to any setting
- **No contact**: Safe for mixed ages
- **Endless rallies**: Keep the ball alive together!

## Equipment Options

### Essential
- Volleyball (regulation or softer beginner ball)
- Open space (at least 20x20 feet)

### Helpful But Optional
- Net (can use rope, string, or nothing!)
- Court lines (chalk, cones, or towels)
- Knee pads (for diving)

### Ball Selection

| Age/Level | Ball Type |
|-----------|-----------|
| Under 8 | Beach ball or soft foam |
| 8-12 | Volley-lite or trainer |
| 12+ | Official volleyball |

## Warm-Up Circuit (5 minutes)

### Volleyball Movement Prep

**Jog & Pass (2 minutes)**
- Partner toss while jogging
- Easy catches and throws
- Gets arms and legs ready

**Arm Circles (30 seconds)**
- 15 forward, 15 backward
- Loosens shoulders for hitting

**Jumping Prep (1 minute)**
- 10 small hops
- 10 medium jumps
- 5 squat jumps
- 5 split jumps

**Quick Feet (1.5 minutes)**
- Shuffle right 10 steps
- Shuffle left 10 steps
- Forward-back quick steps
- Ready position holds

## Station 1: The Bump (Forearm Pass)

The most fundamental skill‚Äîpassing the ball using forearms.

### Proper Form

**Platform Position:**
1. Arms straight out front
2. Hands together (one hand cupped in other)
3. Thumbs side by side, pointing down
4. Create flat surface with forearms

**Body Position:**
1. Feet shoulder-width apart
2. Knees bent, low stance
3. Weight on balls of feet
4. Lean forward slightly

**Contact Point:**
- Ball hits fleshy part of forearms
- Above wrists, below elbows
- Both arms contact at same time
- Push with legs, not arms!

### Bump Drills

**Self-Bump:**
1. Toss ball above yourself
2. Bump straight up
3. Catch it
4. Repeat 10 times

**Partner Toss & Bump:**
1. Partner tosses underhand
2. Bump back to them
3. Start close (6 feet)
4. Move back as skill improves
5. 10 reps, switch

**Wall Bumps:**
1. Face wall (4-6 feet away)
2. Bump ball to wall
3. Let bounce, bump again
4. Goal: consecutive bumps

**Family Bump Circle:**
1. Stand in a circle
2. Bump to anyone in circle
3. Count consecutive bumps
4. Beat your record!

## Station 2: The Set (Overhead Pass)

Setting positions the ball for attack‚Äîrequires soft hands!

### Proper Form

**Hand Position:**
1. Hands above forehead
2. Form triangle with thumbs and fingers
3. Ball contacts all 10 fingers
4. Fingers spread wide, relaxed

**Body Position:**
1. Square to target
2. Knees slightly bent
3. Eyes on the ball
4. Balanced, stable base

**The Push:**
1. Extend arms upward
2. Push through legs and arms
3. Follow through toward target
4. Quick finger snap releases ball

### Setting Drills

**Self-Set:**
1. Toss ball above head
2. Set straight up
3. Catch it
4. Repeat 10 times

**Wall Sets:**
1. Face wall closely
2. Set continuously to wall
3. Keep ball height consistent
4. Count your streak!

**Partner Setting:**
1. Stand 8 feet apart
2. Set back and forth
3. Focus on height (10+ feet)
4. 20 consecutive sets goal

**Set & Move:**
1. Set to partner
2. Shuffle to new position
3. Partner sets back
4. Continuous movement

## Station 3: The Serve

Getting the ball in play starts every point!

### Underhand Serve (Beginners)

**Steps:**
1. Stand behind baseline
2. Hold ball in non-dominant hand
3. Swing hitting arm back
4. Contact ball with heel of hand
5. Follow through toward target

**Tips:**
- Toss ball slightly forward
- Keep eyes on ball
- Step with opposite foot
- Aim for middle of court first

### Overhand Serve (Intermediate)

**Steps:**
1. Toss ball above hitting shoulder
2. Draw arm back (bow and arrow)
3. Contact ball with open palm
4. Snap wrist over top of ball
5. Follow through across body

### Serving Targets

Set up targets on opposite side:
- Towels in different zones
- Cones at corners
- Hula hoops as goals

**Serving Game:**
- 1 point: Over net in bounds
- 3 points: Hit a target
- 5 points: Corner target

## Station 4: The Attack (Spike)

The exciting finish‚Äîslamming the ball over!

### Approach Pattern

**3-Step Approach (Right-handed):**
1. Left foot step (slow)
2. Right-left quick steps (fast)
3. Jump off both feet
4. Arms swing up for lift

### Arm Swing

1. Both arms up during jump
2. Non-hitting arm pulls down
3. Hitting arm draws back
4. Contact ball at highest point
5. Snap wrist over ball
6. Follow through

### Spike Drills

**Self-Toss Spike:**
1. Stand at net (or imaginary line)
2. Toss ball above yourself
3. Jump and hit down
4. Work on timing

**Partner Set & Spike:**
1. Partner sets ball high
2. Approach and spike
3. Focus on timing approach
4. 5 attempts, switch

**Balloon Spikes:**
For beginners‚Äîballoons float longer:
1. Partner tosses balloon
2. Approach and "spike"
3. Perfect for timing practice

## Family Games (15 minutes)

### Newcomb (Catch Volleyball)

Perfect for beginners!
1. Catch ball instead of bumping
2. Throw over net
3. Play regular scoring rules
4. Bridges gap to full volleyball

### Cooperative Rally Count

1. One team on each side (or same side)
2. Rally back and forth
3. Count consecutive passes
4. Ball hits ground = start over
5. Beat your family record!

**Goals:**
- Beginner: 10 rally
- Intermediate: 25 rally
- Advanced: 50 rally

### King of the Beach

1. Two players per side
2. Rally until ball drops
3. Winning side stays
4. New challenger enters
5. Track wins!

### Four Square Volleyball

1. Divide court into 4 squares
2. One person per square
3. Bump/set to other squares
4. Ball drops in your square = point against
5. Rotate positions

### Backyard Volleyball

**Modified Rules:**
- Smaller court (rope boundaries)
- Unlimited hits per side
- Ball can bounce once
- Everyone must touch ball before sending over
- Play to 11 points

## Modifications

### For Younger Kids (5-8)
- Use beach ball or balloon
- Lower net height (rope at chest level)
- Catch and throw allowed
- Bigger court = more success
- Celebrate all contacts!

### For Mixed Ages
- Adults set up easy balls for kids
- Kids get extra hits
- Position weaker players near net
- Rotate serving duty
- Team success focus

### No Net Version
- Circle keep-up
- Wall rally practice
- Triangle passing
- Self-skill challenges
- All skills still develop!

## Pro Tips

1. **Bend knees, not back**: Power from legs
2. **Platform angle**: Aim with your platform direction
3. **Move feet first**: Get in position, then play ball
4. **Communicate**: Call "mine!" on every ball
5. **Stay low**: Ready position is athletic stance
6. **Soft hands for setting**: Let ball sink into fingers
7. **Follow through**: Direction = where you finish
