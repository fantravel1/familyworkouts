# Tennis Skills for Families

Grab your rackets and head to the court! Tennis is an incredible family sport that builds coordination, fitness, and lifelong skills.

## Why Tennis for Families?

- **Lifetime sport**: Play from age 4 to 94
- **Full-body workout**: Every muscle group engaged
- **Mental challenge**: Strategy keeps minds sharp
- **Social bonding**: Doubles brings families together
- **Accessible**: Public courts are everywhere

## Equipment Needed

### Essential
- Tennis rackets (sized appropriatelyâ€”see guide below)
- Tennis balls (pressureless for beginners last longer)
- Court access (many parks have free courts)

## Warm-Up: Court Fitness (5 minutes)

### Dynamic Warm-Up

**Sideline to Sideline (2 minutes)**
- Jog across court width
- High knees back
- Butt kicks across
- Skipping back
- Side shuffles both directions
- Carioca (grapevine)

**Arm Circles (1 minute)**
- Small circles forward (30 seconds)
- Large circles backward (30 seconds)
- Shoulder rolls

**Shadow Swings (2 minutes)**
- Forehand motion x10
- Backhand motion x10
- Serve motion x10
- No racket neededâ€”focus on form

## Station 1: Grip & Ready Position (4 minutes)

### The Continental Grip
*Used for serves and volleys*

1. Hold racket like shaking hands
2. V between thumb and index finger on top bevel
3. Firm but not tight grip
4. Practice switching between grips

### Eastern Forehand Grip
*Used for basic groundstrokes*

1. Place palm on racket face
2. Slide hand down to handle
3. Natural handshake position

### Ready Position
- Feet shoulder-width apart
- Knees slightly bent
- Racket in front, both hands
- Weight on balls of feet
- Eyes on the ball!

**Family Challenge**: See who can hold ready position longest (like a wall sit for tennis!)

## Station 2: Bounce & Hit (6 minutes)

Start with the fundamentals before rallying.

### Self-Drop Forehand

1. Stand sideways to net
2. Drop ball from waist height
3. Let it bounce once
4. Swing low to high
5. Follow through over shoulder

**Reps**: 10 each family member

### Self-Drop Backhand

1. Turn opposite side to net
2. Two hands on racket (for beginners)
3. Drop ball in front
4. Swing through contact point
5. Follow through across body

**Reps**: 10 each family member

### Key Phrases
- "Shake hands with the racket"
- "Low to high"
- "Watch the ball hit strings"
- "Point with your shoulder"

## Station 3: Wall Rally (5 minutes)

A wall is a perfect practice partner!

### Basic Wall Rally

1. Stand 10 feet from wall
2. Hit ball softly against wall
3. Let it bounce once
4. Hit again
5. Count consecutive hits

**Family Competition**: Who can rally longest?

| Level | Goal |
|-------|------|
| Beginner | 5 in a row |
| Intermediate | 15 in a row |
| Advanced | 30 in a row |

### Wall Volley Challenge

1. Stand closer (6 feet)
2. Hit before ball bounces
3. Requires quick reflexes
4. Builds hand-eye coordination

## Station 4: Mini Tennis (6 minutes)

Use the service boxes for family rallies.

### Setup
- One player per service box
- Rally using only half the court
- Great for control development

### Rules
- Ball must bounce in service box
- One bounce allowed
- Cooperative counting (team goal)
- Then competitive points

**Family Goals**:
- 10 rally cooperative
- 20 rally cooperative
- 30 rally = pizza celebration!

### Progression
1. Underhand tosses first
2. Gentle hits with racket
3. Normal pace rallies
4. Add movement (stay in box)

## Station 5: Serve Introduction (5 minutes)

The serve starts every pointâ€”let's learn it!

### Beginner Serve (Platform)

**The Simplified Serve:**
1. Stand sideways at baseline
2. Toss ball straight up (just above head)
3. Reach up and forward
4. Hit ball into service box diagonally

**Practice Sequence:**
- Toss practice only (10 tosses)
- Reach and catch (practice motion)
- Hit into fence first (for rhythm)
- Aim at service box

### Serving Targets

Set up targets in service box:
- Cones in corners
- Empty ball cans
- Chalk circles

**Family Game**: Bowling for serves!
- Knock down targets = points
- Take turns serving
- Most targets wins

## Station 6: Footwork Drills (4 minutes)

Tennis is all about movement!

### Alley Sprints
- Sprint down doubles alley
- Side shuffle back
- Repeat 4 times

### Box Shuffle
- Use service box as grid
- Shuffle to each corner
- Touch ground
- Return to center
- Random call-outs

### Split Step Practice
- Hop and land on balls of feet
- React and move in called direction
- Essential for match play
- Do 20 split steps

## Family Games (10 minutes)

### King/Queen of the Court

1. One player on each side of net
2. Rally until someone misses
3. Winner stays, challenger enters
4. Track who stays longest

### Around the World

1. All players on same side
2. Hit and run to other side
3. Circle around the court
4. Miss = lose a life (3 lives)

### Tennis Baseball

1. Server tosses ball
2. Batter hits and runs bases (cones at corners)
3. Fielders try to catch
4. Caught = out
5. 3 outs switch sides

### Target Practice

Place targets around the court:
- 1 point: Anywhere in bounds
- 3 points: Service boxes
- 5 points: Inside cone circles
- 10 points: Knock over water bottle

## Cool-Down Stretches (5 minutes)

### Shoulder Stretch
- Arm across body
- Hold 20 seconds each side

### Forearm Stretch
- Extend arm, pull fingers back
- Prevents tennis elbow
- 20 seconds each arm

### Hip Flexor Lunge
- Deep lunge position
- Hold 30 seconds each side

### Calf Stretch
- Against fence or wall
- Back leg straight
- 20 seconds each leg

### Wrist Circles
- 10 circles each direction
- Each wrist

## Progress Tracker

### Beginner Goals
- [ ] Hold racket correctly
- [ ] 5 consecutive wall hits
- [ ] Get 3 serves in the box
- [ ] Rally 5 times with partner

### Intermediate Goals
- [ ] 15 wall hits in a row
- [ ] 10 cooperative rallies
- [ ] Hit both corners with serve
- [ ] Play one full game

### Advanced Goals
- [ ] 30+ wall volley streak
- [ ] Win a game using strategy
- [ ] Consistent serve (7/10)
- [ ] Play a full match

## Tips for Tennis Parents

1. **Start slow**: Control before power
2. **Short sessions**: 30-45 minutes max for kids
3. **Make it fun**: Games over drills
4. **Celebrate progress**: Not just wins
5. **Play together**: Mixed doubles is magic
6. **Stay positive**: Tennis can be frustrating
7. **Hydrate**: Courts get hot!

## Rainy Day Practice

Can't get to the court? Try these at home:

- **Shadow swings**: Practice motion without ball
- **Wall practice**: Garage or basement wall
- **Balloon tennis**: Gentle indoor rallies
- **Balance drills**: Ready position holds
- **Grip practice**: Switch between grips
- **Watch tennis**: Learn by observation

## Family Tennis Traditions

- **Sunday court time**: Weekly family session
- **Grand Slam watch parties**: Watch the pros together
- **Tennis vacation**: Visit a tennis resort
- **League play**: Join family doubles leagues
- **Annual tournament**: Family championship day
