## Hoops for Everyone!

Basketball is one of the best family activities‚Äîit's active, skill-based, and endlessly adaptable. Whether you have a driveway hoop, park court, or even a laundry basket inside, these drills work for everyone!

## What You'll Need

- Basketball (size appropriate‚Äîsmaller for young kids)
- Hoop of any kind (adjustable height ideal)
- Flat surface
- Optional: Cones or markers

## Why Basketball for Families?

- **Scalable difficulty**: Lower hoop, smaller ball for little ones
- **Turn-based**: Natural rest periods
- **Life skills**: Teaches teamwork, practice, perseverance
- **Indoor/outdoor**: Adaptable to any weather
- **All ages**: Grandma can shoot too!

## Warm-Up: Ball Handling (5 minutes)

### Ball Slaps
- Pass ball quickly between hands
- Slap it back and forth
- 30 seconds
- Warms up hands and arms

### Around the World
- Circle ball around head
- Circle ball around waist
- Circle ball around knees
- Circle ball around ankles
- 5 circles each direction at each level

### Figure 8
- Legs apart
- Weave ball through legs in figure 8
- Don't let it touch the ground!
- 30 seconds

### Ball Bounces
- Bounce and catch at different heights
- High bounce, medium bounce, low bounce
- Switch hands
- 1 minute total

---

## Skill Station 1: Dribbling (5 minutes)

### Stationary Dribble
- Stand still, dribble right hand
- 20 dribbles
- Switch to left hand
- 20 dribbles
- "Push the ball down, don't slap it!"

### Walking Dribble
- Dribble while walking slowly
- Right hand only first
- Then left hand only
- Finally, alternate hands
- 2 minutes around the court

### Cone Weave (or Shoe Weave)
- Set up 5 markers in a line
- Dribble weaving through them
- Go slow for control
- Speed up as you improve
- Both directions!

### Dribble Tag
- Everyone dribbles
- One person is "it"
- Tag others while dribbling
- If you lose your dribble, you're frozen
- Another dribbler can unfreeze you

---

## Skill Station 2: Passing (5 minutes)

### Chest Pass
- Face partner, 8 feet apart
- Ball at chest, thumbs behind ball
- Push forward, snap wrists
- Ball should arrive at partner's chest
- 10 passes each

### Bounce Pass
- Same position
- Bounce ball 2/3 of the way to partner
- Ball should bounce up to partner's waist
- 10 passes each

### Overhead Pass
- Ball above head
- Throw to partner's head level
- "Like you're throwing a soccer throw-in!"
- 10 passes each

### Triangle Passing
- Three family members form triangle
- Pass around the triangle
- Call the person's name before passing!
- Both directions

### Hot Potato
- Everyone in a circle
- Pass ball randomly around circle
- If music stops (or someone yells "STOP"), person with ball does 5 jumping jacks
- Fast pace!

---

## Skill Station 3: Shooting (10 minutes)

### Form Shooting (No Hoop)
- Stand 3 feet from wall
- Practice shooting motion into wall
- B-E-E-F:
  - **B**alance: Feet shoulder-width
  - **E**lbow: Under the ball
  - **E**yes: On the target
  - **F**ollow-through: Snap wrist, fingers down

### Close Shots
- Stand right under basket
- Focus on form, not distance
- 10 shots each person
- Celebrate makes!

### Free Throw Practice
- Standard free throw line (or closer for young kids)
- Each person shoots 5 in a row
- Count family total makes
- "Let's beat yesterday's score!"

### HORSE (or PIG)
- One person makes a shot
- Everyone else must copy exact shot
- Miss = get a letter
- Spell HORSE = you're out
- Creative shots encouraged!

### Around the World
- 5 spots around the key
- Make a shot to advance
- Miss = stay at that spot
- First one "around the world" wins

---

## Skill Station 4: Team Games (5 minutes)

### Knockout
- Line up at free throw line
- First two people have balls
- First person shoots
- Second person tries to make shot BEFORE first person
- If second person makes it first, first person is "knocked out"
- Fast-paced, exciting!

### 21
- Everyone shoots from anywhere
- Regular shot = 2 points
- Three-pointer = 3 points
- Free throw after make = 1 point
- First to exactly 21 wins
- Go over 21 = back to 15!

### Family Shootout
- Each person picks their spot
- 60 seconds on the clock
- Take as many shots as possible
- Count total family makes
- Try to beat your family record!

## Cool-Down (5 minutes)

### Ball Stretches
- Seated, roll ball around feet
- Seated, roll ball side to side while stretching
- Standing, ball overhead side bends

### Leg Stretches
- Quad stretch (hold ankle)
- Hamstring stretch (foot on ball)
- Calf stretch against wall

### Arm Circles
- Big circles forward
- Big circles backward

### Deep Breaths
- "Great practice today!"
- Discuss one thing each person improved on

## Age Adaptations

### Young Kids (4-7)
- Smaller ball (size 3)
- Lower hoop (6-7 feet)
- Focus on fun over form
- More games, less drills
- Celebrate every basket!

### Kids (8-12)
- Youth ball (size 5-6)
- Standard or slightly lower hoop
- Can learn proper form
- Add competition elements
- They'll want to practice more!

### Teens
- Regulation ball and hoop
- More intense drills
- One-on-one games
- May school the parents!
- Let them teach younger siblings

### Adults
- Focus on fundamentals (it's humbling!)
- Great cardio workout
- Model good sportsmanship
- Don't let kids win every time, but also don't dominate

## Indoor Alternatives

**No hoop? No problem!**

- Laundry basket on chair = hoop
- Rolled up socks = ball
- All dribbling and passing drills work with any ball
- Balloon basketball for toddlers
- Nerf hoops for serious indoor play

## Basketball Life Lessons

Use practice time to discuss:
- **Practice matters**: Skills improve with repetition
- **Everyone contributes**: Different roles, same team
- **Mistakes happen**: Miss a shot? Take another
- **Support each other**: Cheer teammates, encourage family
- **Have fun**: The real goal!
