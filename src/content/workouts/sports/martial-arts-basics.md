# Martial Arts Basics for Families

Discover the world of martial arts together! Learn foundational moves from various styles while building strength, coordination, discipline, and confidence.

## The Martial Arts Way

Martial arts teaches more than fighting:
- **Respect**: For self and others
- **Discipline**: Mental focus and self-control
- **Confidence**: Knowing you can protect yourself
- **Fitness**: Full-body conditioning
- **Focus**: Mind-body connection

## Safety First

### Ground Rules

1. **No contact**: Practice in the air only
2. **Space out**: Arm's length apart minimum
3. **Control**: Slow and controlled movements
4. **Stop means stop**: Respect boundaries
5. **No showing off**: Safety over style

### Training Space

- Clear area of obstacles
- Non-slip surface
- Enough room to kick without hitting anything
- Supervised by adults

## Warm-Up: Martial Arts Style (5 minutes)

### Joint Rotation (2 minutes)

**Neck Circles**
- 5 circles each direction
- Slow and controlled

**Shoulder Circles**
- 10 forward, 10 backward
- Arms loose at sides

**Hip Circles**
- Hands on hips
- 10 circles each direction

**Knee Circles**
- Feet together, hands on knees
- 10 circles each direction

**Ankle Circles**
- 10 each direction, each ankle

### Dynamic Stretches (3 minutes)

**Leg Swings** (30 seconds each leg)
- Hold wall for balance
- Swing leg front to back
- Swing leg side to side

**Arm Swings** (30 seconds)
- Swing arms across body
- Open wide, cross over

**Torso Twists** (30 seconds)
- Arms relaxed
- Twist side to side
- Let arms follow

**Jumping Jacks** (30 seconds)
- Get heart rate up
- Ready for training!

**Light Jog in Place** (30 seconds)
- Stay loose
- Bounce lightly

## Section 1: Stances (4 minutes)

### Ready Stance (Attention)
1. Feet together
2. Arms at sides
3. Eyes forward
4. Stand tall
5. *This shows respect and focus*

### Fighting Stance
1. Feet shoulder-width apart
2. One foot forward (comfortable side)
3. Knees slightly bent
4. Hands up protecting face
5. Elbows protect ribs
6. Chin tucked slightly

### Horse Stance
1. Feet wide, double shoulder-width
2. Toes pointing forward
3. Bend knees into squat
4. Back straight
5. Hold 15-30 seconds
6. *Builds leg strength!*

### Cat Stance
1. Most weight on back leg
2. Front foot light, ready to kick
3. Knees bent
4. Good for quick front kicks

## Section 2: Hand Techniques (6 minutes)

### Making a Proper Fist
1. Curl fingers into palm
2. Thumb wraps outside
3. Wrist straight
4. Never tuck thumb inside!

### Basic Punch (Jab)
1. Start in fighting stance
2. Front hand punches straight out
3. Rotate fist (palm down at extension)
4. Pull back quickly
5. Other hand stays protecting face

**Practice:** 10 jabs each side

### Cross Punch
1. Start in fighting stance
2. Back hand punches forward
3. Rotate hips with punch
4. Power comes from hips!
5. Return to guard

**Practice:** 10 crosses each side

### Combination: Jab-Cross
1. Jab with front hand
2. Cross with back hand
3. Return to guard
4. Smooth and fast

**Practice:** 10 combinations

### Basic Block (High Block)
1. Start with arm across body
2. Raise arm up at angle
3. Forearm protects head
4. Imagine blocking overhead attack

**Practice:** 10 each arm

### Inside Block
1. Start with arm across body
2. Sweep outward
3. Forearm deflects incoming strike
4. Protect your center

**Practice:** 10 each arm

## Section 3: Kicking Basics (6 minutes)

### Front Kick
1. Start in fighting stance
2. Raise knee high
3. Extend foot forward (ball of foot strikes)
4. Pull leg back to knee position
5. Return to stance

**Key Points:**
- Chamber the knee first
- Kick in a straight line
- Re-chamber before setting down

**Practice:** 10 each leg

### Side Kick
1. Start in fighting stance
2. Raise knee (turn hips)
3. Extend foot sideways (heel or edge strikes)
4. Push with hip
5. Return to stance

**Key Points:**
- Look toward target
- Keep standing leg slightly bent
- Balance is crucial

**Practice:** 8 each leg

### Roundhouse Kick
1. Start in fighting stance
2. Raise knee to side
3. Rotate hips, swing leg in arc
4. Strike with top of foot or shin
5. Return to stance

**Key Points:**
- Pivot on standing foot
- Hip rotation provides power
- Control the arc

**Practice:** 8 each leg

### Back Kick (Basic)
1. Start in fighting stance
2. Look over shoulder at target
3. Raise knee
4. Push heel straight back
5. Return to stance

**Key Points:**
- Keep looking at target
- Stay balanced
- Push straight back

**Practice:** 6 each leg

## Section 4: Combinations (5 minutes)

### Combo 1: Basic Defense
- Block (high block)
- Counter punch (cross)
- Repeat 5 times each side

### Combo 2: Attack Sequence
- Jab - Cross - Front kick
- Repeat 5 times each side

### Combo 3: Multi-kick
- Front kick (front leg)
- Roundhouse kick (back leg)
- Repeat 5 times each side

### Combo 4: Full Sequence
- Jab - Cross - High block - Front kick - Back to fighting stance
- Repeat 5 times

### Family Challenge
- Create your own 4-move combination
- Teach it to the family
- Everyone learns everyone's combo!

## Section 5: Partner Drills (4 minutes)

*Note: These are NO-CONTACT drills!*

### Mirror Drill
1. Partners face each other
2. One leads, one mirrors
3. Go through stances and techniques
4. Switch leaders

### Reaction Game
1. Partner holds up hands as targets
2. Other aims punches/kicks toward (NOT hitting!)
3. Practice accuracy and control
4. Stop 6 inches away from hands

## Cool-Down: Meditation Moment (3 minutes)

### Breathing Exercise
1. Sit cross-legged (seiza or easy pose)
2. Hands on knees
3. Eyes closed
4. Deep breaths through nose
5. 1 minute of silence

### Martial Arts Wisdom
Share one of these quotes:

### Bow Out
1. Stand in ready stance
2. Bow to each family member
3. Say "Thank you for training"
4. Formal end to practice

## Stretching (3 minutes)

### Leg Stretches

**Seated Forward Fold**
- Legs extended
- Reach for toes
- Hold 30 seconds

**Straddle Stretch**
- Legs wide apart
- Lean forward
- Hold 30 seconds

**Butterfly Stretch**
- Soles of feet together
- Gently press knees down
- Hold 30 seconds

### Hip Flexor Stretch
- Lunge position
- Back knee down
- Push hips forward
- 30 seconds each side

### Shoulder Stretch
- Arm across body
- Hold at elbow
- 20 seconds each arm

## Safety Reminders

### Do:
- Practice with control
- Stay hydrated
- Warm up properly
- Use proper form
- Stop if anything hurts

### Don't:
- Hit or make contact
- Practice when tired or angry
- Skip warm-up
- Show off with high kicks too soon
- Use techniques on siblings (ever!)
