# Bodyweight Basics for the Whole Family

Build real strength with zero equipment! This progressive workout teaches fundamental movement patterns that everyone should master.

## The Bodyweight Advantage

- **Free forever**: No gym membership needed
- **Anywhere, anytime**: Hotel room, backyard, living room
- **Functional strength**: Movements you use in daily life
- **Self-regulating**: Your body weight = your challenge
- **Safe for all ages**: Natural movements, lower injury risk

## The 6 Fundamental Movements

Every strength program should include:

1. **Push** - Push-up variations
2. **Pull** - Row and pull-up progressions
3. **Squat** - Sitting/standing patterns
4. **Hinge** - Bending at the hips
5. **Core** - Stability and rotation
6. **Carry/Balance** - Loaded movement

## Warm-Up: Movement Prep (4 minutes)

### Joint Circles (2 minutes)

**Neck Circles**
- 5 slow circles each direction
- Gentle, controlled movement

**Arm Circles**
- 10 small forward
- 10 large forward
- Repeat backward

**Hip Circles**
- Hands on hips
- 10 circles each direction
- Like hula hooping!

**Ankle Circles**
- 10 each direction per ankle
- Lift foot off ground

### Dynamic Stretches (2 minutes)

**Leg Swings**
- 10 forward/back each leg
- 10 side to side each leg
- Hold something for balance

**Torso Twists**
- Arms out, twist left/right
- 20 total twists
- Feet stay planted

**Inchworms**
- 5 total
- Walk hands out to plank
- Walk feet to hands
- Loosen up that whole body!

## Station 1: Push Progressions (4 minutes)

### Level 1: Wall Push-Ups
*Best for: Beginners, young kids*

1. Face wall, arm's length away
2. Hands flat on wall, shoulder width
3. Lean forward, chest toward wall
4. Push back to start
5. **Reps: 10-15**

### Level 2: Incline Push-Ups
*Best for: Building strength*

1. Hands on sturdy surface (couch, stairs)
2. Body straight from head to heels
3. Lower chest to surface
4. Push back up
5. **Reps: 8-12**

### Level 3: Knee Push-Ups
*Best for: Almost there!*

1. Hands on floor, knees down
2. Body straight from head to knees
3. Lower chest to floor
4. Push back up
5. **Reps: 8-12**

### Level 4: Full Push-Ups
*Best for: Ready for the real deal*

1. Hands and toes on floor
2. Body straight as a plank
3. Lower chest to floor
4. Push back up with control
5. **Reps: 5-15**

### Level 5: Advanced Variations
*Best for: Push-up masters*

- Diamond push-ups (hands together)
- Wide push-ups (hands extra wide)
- Archer push-ups (shift side to side)
- Decline push-ups (feet elevated)
- **Reps: 5-10**

**Do 2-3 sets at YOUR level!**

## Station 2: Squat Progressions (4 minutes)

### Level 1: Sit-to-Stand
*Best for: Building basics*

1. Sit on sturdy chair
2. Stand up without using hands
3. Sit back down with control
4. **Reps: 10-15**

### Level 2: Assisted Squat
*Best for: Learning form*

1. Hold onto something sturdy
2. Feet shoulder-width apart
3. Squat down, keeping heels down
4. Stand back up
5. **Reps: 10-15**

### Level 3: Bodyweight Squat
*Best for: The fundamental movement*

1. Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out
2. Arms forward for balance
3. Squat until thighs parallel
4. Keep chest up, heels down
5. **Reps: 10-20**

### Level 4: Squat Variations
*Best for: Adding challenge*

- Sumo squat (wide stance)
- Narrow squat (feet together)
- Pause squat (hold at bottom)
- Pulse squat (small bounces)
- **Reps: 10-15**

### Level 5: Single Leg Progress
*Best for: Advanced strength*

- Split squat (one foot forward)
- Bulgarian split squat (rear foot elevated)
- Pistol squat progressions
- **Reps: 5-10 each leg**

**Do 2-3 sets at YOUR level!**

## Station 3: Pull Progressions (4 minutes)

*These require a sturdy horizontal bar, table, or door*

### Level 1: Door Rows
*Best for: Beginners*

1. Open sturdy door, stand at edge
2. Hold both door handles
3. Lean back, arms straight
4. Pull chest toward door
5. **Reps: 10-15**

### Level 2: Table Rows
*Best for: Building pulling strength*

1. Lie under sturdy table
2. Grab table edge
3. Pull chest up toward table
4. Lower with control
5. **Reps: 8-12**

### Level 3: Incline Rows
*Best for: Progressing toward pull-ups*

1. Bar at waist height
2. Walk feet forward
3. Body straight, pull chest to bar
4. Lower with control
5. **Reps: 8-12**

### Level 4: Full Inverted Rows
*Best for: Advanced pulling*

1. Body horizontal under bar
2. Feet on floor or elevated
3. Pull chest to bar
4. Straight line from head to heels
5. **Reps: 5-12**

### Level 5: Pull-Up Progressions
*Best for: The ultimate pull*

- Dead hangs (just holding)
- Negative pull-ups (lower slowly)
- Band-assisted pull-ups
- Full pull-ups
- **Reps: 1-10**

**Do 2-3 sets at YOUR level!**

## Station 4: Hinge Progressions (3 minutes)

### Level 1: Wall Hip Hinge
*Best for: Learning the pattern*

1. Stand arm's length from wall
2. Feet hip-width apart
3. Push hips back until glutes touch wall
4. Keep back straight, knees slightly bent
5. **Reps: 10-15**

### Level 2: Good Mornings
*Best for: Building hamstring strength*

1. Hands behind head
2. Hinge forward at hips
3. Back stays straight
4. Feel stretch in hamstrings
5. **Reps: 10-15**

### Level 3: Romanian Deadlift
*Best for: Full hinge pattern*

1. Stand tall, hands in front of thighs
2. Push hips back, hands slide down legs
3. Lower until hamstrings stretch
4. Squeeze glutes to stand
5. **Reps: 10-15**

### Level 4: Single Leg Deadlift
*Best for: Balance + strength*

1. Stand on one leg
2. Hinge forward, back leg rises
3. Body forms a T shape
4. Return with control
5. **Reps: 6-10 each leg**

**Do 2 sets at YOUR level!**

## Station 5: Core Progressions (4 minutes)

### Front Core (Plank Family)

**Level 1: Knee Plank**
Hold 15-30 seconds

**Level 2: Full Plank**
Hold 20-45 seconds

**Level 3: Plank Shoulder Taps**
10-20 taps total

**Level 4: Plank Up-Downs**
6-12 total (plank to push-up position)

### Side Core (Side Plank Family)

**Level 1: Side Plank on Knees**
Hold 15-30 seconds each side

**Level 2: Full Side Plank**
Hold 20-45 seconds each side

**Level 3: Side Plank with Reach**
6-10 reaches each side

### Rotational Core

**Dead Bug** (All Levels)
1. Lie on back, arms and legs up
2. Lower opposite arm and leg
3. Return, switch sides
4. Keep lower back pressed down!
5. **Reps: 10-16 total**

### Back Core

**Bird Dog** (All Levels)
1. Hands and knees on floor
2. Extend opposite arm and leg
3. Hold briefly, return
4. Switch sides
5. **Reps: 10-16 total**

**Do 2 sets of core exercises!**

## Station 6: Balance & Carry (2 minutes)

### Single Leg Stance
- Stand on one foot
- 20-30 seconds each side
- Close eyes for challenge!

### Walking Lunges
- 10 steps forward
- 10 steps back
- Keep torso upright

### Bear Crawl
- Hands and feet on ground
- Crawl forward 20 steps
- Crawl backward 20 steps

## Cool-Down Stretches (4 minutes)

### Upper Body

**Chest Stretch (30 sec each side)**
- Arm against wall
- Turn body away
- Stretch front of shoulder/chest

**Tricep Stretch (30 sec each)**
- Arm overhead, hand toward back
- Gently press elbow

### Lower Body

**Quad Stretch (30 sec each)**
- Pull foot to glutes
- Keep knees together
- Stand tall

**Hamstring Stretch (30 sec each)**
- Foot on low surface
- Hinge forward at hips
- Feel stretch down back of leg

**Calf Stretch (30 sec each)**
- Step back, press heel down
- Lean into wall or object

### Full Body

**Child's Pose (30 sec)**
- Knees wide, arms forward
- Sink hips toward heels
- Breathe deeply

**Final Deep Breaths (30 sec)**
- Stand tall
- Arms overhead, big inhale
- Exhale, lower arms
- Feel accomplished!

## Weekly Progression Plan

### Week 1-2: Foundation
- 2 sessions per week
- 2 sets per station
- Focus on form over reps

### Week 3-4: Building
- 3 sessions per week
- 3 sets per station
- Increase reps slightly

### Week 5-6: Progressing
- 3 sessions per week
- Try next level exercises
- Track improvements

### Week 7-8: Challenging
- 3-4 sessions per week
- Advanced variations
- Test max reps/holds

## Form Cues Everyone Should Know

### Push
- "Elbows at 45 degrees"
- "Squeeze your abs"
- "Straight line head to heels"

### Squat
- "Sit back like there's a chair"
- "Knees track over toes"
- "Chest up, proud posture"

### Plank
- "Glutes squeezed"
- "Belly button to spine"
- "Don't let hips sag"

### Hinge
- "Push hips back"
- "Flat back, not rounded"
- "Feel hamstrings stretch"

## Pro Tips

1. **Progress slowly**: Master one level before advancing
2. **Form first**: Good form beats more reps
3. **Rest appropriately**: 30-60 seconds between sets
4. **Stay consistent**: 2-3x per week minimum
5. **Scale individually**: Family members at different levels = OK!
