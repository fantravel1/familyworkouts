## Stronger Together

Who needs a gym when you have each other? Partner exercises add fun, accountability, and natural resistance to strength training. Plus, you'll bond while you build muscle!

## Benefits of Partner Training

- **Built-in spotter**: Safety first!
- **Motivation**: Harder to quit with a partner
- **Variable resistance**: Partners adjust to your level
- **Social**: More fun than solo workouts
- **Accountability**: You show up for each other

## Pairing Guidelines

**Best matches:**
- Parent + teen
- Similar-sized siblings
- Adult partners
- Parent + 2 kids (some exercises)

**Size differences are okay!** Adjust exercises accordingly.

## Warm-Up Together (3 minutes)

### Mirror March
- Face each other
- One person leads, other mirrors
- Include: high knees, arm swings, squats
- 1 minute each as leader

### Partner Arm Circles
- Stand facing each other, arms out
- Palms touching partner's palms
- Circle arms together forward 10x
- Circle arms together backward 10x

### Back-to-Back Twist
- Stand back to back
- Pass a ball (or pretend to) around your bodies
- 10 passes each direction
- Loosens spine, warms core

## Main Workout

### Exercise 1: Partner Squats
**3 sets of 12 reps**

**How to do it:**
1. Face each other, hold hands or wrists
2. Both squat down together
3. Use each other for balance
4. Stand up together
5. Keep chests up!

**Challenge version:**
- Single leg squat (pistol) holding partner's hands

**Why it works:**
- Balance assistance allows deeper squats
- Synchronized movement builds teamwork
- Core engaged throughout

---

### Exercise 2: Wheelbarrow Walk + Push-Ups
**2 sets of 10 steps + 5 push-ups**

**How to do it:**
1. Person A: Plank position
2. Person B: Hold Person A's ankles
3. Person A walks on hands forward 10 "steps"
4. Person A does 5 push-ups while B holds legs
5. Switch roles!

**Modification:**
- Hold at thighs instead of ankles (easier)
- Fewer steps and push-ups for beginners

**Why it works:**
- Upper body and core strength for Person A
- Grip and stability for Person B
- Serious giggles guaranteed!

---

### Exercise 3: Partner Plank High-Fives
**3 sets of 20 high-fives**

**How to do it:**
1. Both in plank position, facing each other
2. Lift right hand, high-five partner
3. Return to plank
4. Lift left hand, high-five
5. Continue alternating

**Modification:**
- Knees down
- High-five less frequently
- One person holds plank, other does high-fives

**Why it works:**
- Core stability
- Shoulder strength
- Coordination and timing

---

### Exercise 4: Sit-Up Pass
**3 sets of 10 passes each**

**How to do it:**
1. Lie on backs, feet interlocked
2. One person holds ball (or pillow)
3. Both sit up at same time
4. Pass ball at the top
5. Both lie back down
6. Other person sits up and passes back

**Modification:**
- Feet not interlocked, just touching
- No ball, just high-five at top
- Crunch instead of full sit-up

**Why it works:**
- Core strength
- Timing and coordination
- More fun than solo sit-ups!

---

### Exercise 5: Back-to-Back Wall Sit
**3 rounds: Hold as long as possible**

**How to do it:**
1. Stand back to back
2. Slide down into "chair" position
3. Press into each other's backs for support
4. Hold!
5. Compete: Who gives up first?

**Modification:**
- Higher "sit" position
- Hands on thighs for support

**Why it works:**
- Quad and glute strength
- Mutual support (literally!)
- Mental toughness challenge

---

### Exercise 6: Partner Rows
**3 sets of 10 each person**

**How to do it:**
1. Both stand facing each other
2. Hold each other's wrists
3. Person A leans back, Person B rows them forward
4. Person A creates resistance, Person B pulls
5. 10 reps then switch roles

**Modification:**
- Less lean = less resistance
- Towel or resistance band between partners

**Why it works:**
- Back and bicep strength
- Adjustable resistance
- Trust building!

---

### Exercise 7: Leg Press Push
**3 sets of 10 each leg**

**How to do it:**
1. Person A lies on back, knees bent, feet up
2. Person B stands at A's feet
3. A presses feet against B's hands
4. B provides resistance as A extends legs
5. 10 presses each leg, then switch

**Modification:**
- Less resistance from standing partner
- Both legs at same time

**Why it works:**
- Quad strength (pressing person)
- Core and stability (resistance person)
- Communication skills!

---

### Exercise 8: Resistance Push-Ups
**3 sets of 8**

**How to do it:**
1. Person A in push-up position
2. Person B places hands lightly on A's upper back
3. A does push-up while B provides gentle downward resistance
4. B adjusts pressure based on A's strength
5. Switch after set

**Modification:**
- Knees down
- No resistance (just motivation)

**Why it works:**
- Increased push-up intensity
- Variable resistance
- Real-time feedback

## Cool-Down Together (3 minutes)

### Partner Seated Forward Fold
- Sit facing each other, legs extended
- Hold hands or wrists
- One person leans back, pulling other forward
- Gentle stretch, hold 20 seconds
- Switch roles

### Partner Chest Opener
- Stand back to back
- Link arms at elbows
- One person leans forward, lifting partner gently
- Opens partner's chest
- Very gentle! Switch roles

### Partner Shoulder Stretch
- Stand side by side
- Cross inside arms in front of body
- Partner helps gently pull arm across
- 20 seconds each arm

### Finish
- High-five or fist bump!
- Celebrate together!

## Family Trio Variations

**For 3 people (like 1 adult + 2 kids):**

- **Triangle plank:** All three in plank circle, pass high-fives around
- **Wall sit chain:** All three in line, backs against each other
- **Relay rows:** Two hold, one rows, rotate

---

## Safety Tips

1. **Communicate**: "More resistance" / "Less resistance"
2. **Start light**: Add intensity gradually
3. **Trust carefully**: Know partner's strength level
4. **Spot properly**: Be ready to assist
5. **Listen**: Stop if anyone feels pain

## Weekly Progression

| Week | Sets | Difficulty |
|------|------|------------|
| 1 | 2 sets | Learn form, light resistance |
| 2 | 3 sets | Standard resistance |
| 3 | 3 sets | Increased resistance |
| 4 | 4 sets | Challenge mode! |
