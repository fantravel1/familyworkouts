# Partner Resistance Workout

Your best piece of fitness equipment? Each other! This workout uses partner resistance for an effective, fun, equipment-free strength session.

## The Partner Advantage

- **Adjustable resistance**: Partner controls the challenge
- **Built-in accountability**: Can't skip if partner's waiting!
- **Instant feedback**: Partner watches form
- **More fun**: Exercise is better together
- **Communication practice**: Teamwork skills

## Partner Pairing Tips

### Matching Partners
- Similar size works best for many exercises
- Parent-child works great too!
- Adjust resistance based on strength differences
- Communicate throughout

### Safety Rules
1. Always warm up first
2. Communicate constantly
3. Start with light resistance
4. Stop if anything hurts
5. Have fun!

## Warm-Up: Partner Connection (4 minutes)

### Getting Ready Together

**Mirror March** (1 minute)
- Face each other
- One leads, one mirrors
- March, arm movements
- Switch leaders halfway

**Partner Arm Swings** (30 seconds)
- Stand side by side
- Swing arms together
- Synchronize movement
- Get in rhythm!

**Back-to-Back Twists** (1 minute)
- Stand back to back
- Twist to pass imaginary ball
- Go both directions
- Warm up those spines!

**Partner Squats** (1 minute)
- Hold hands, face each other
- Squat together
- Support each other's balance
- 10 synchronized squats

**High-Five Jacks** (30 seconds)
- Jumping jacks facing each other
- High-five at the top
- Coordination challenge!
- Fun warm-up!

## Upper Body Partners (7 minutes)

### Arms, Chest, Back, Shoulders

**Exercise 1: Resistance Push** (1 minute)

*Partner A pushes, Partner B resists*

Setup:
1. Face each other, arms extended
2. Palms pressing together

Action:
- A pushes forward against B's resistance
- B provides steady resistance (not maximum)
- Switch roles after 30 seconds

*Works: Chest, shoulders, triceps*

---

**Exercise 2: Resistance Pull** (1 minute)

*Partner A pulls, Partner B resists*

Setup:
1. Hold each other's wrists
2. Arms extended between you

Action:
- A pulls arms toward chest
- B provides resistance
- Like a rowing motion
- Switch after 30 seconds

*Works: Back, biceps*

---

**Exercise 3: Shoulder Press Partners** (1 minute)

Setup:
1. A sits on floor
2. B stands behind A
3. B's hands on A's hands (overhead)

Action:
- A presses up against B's downward resistance
- Controlled movement
- Switch positions after 30 seconds

*Works: Shoulders, triceps*

---

**Exercise 4: Partner Push-Up High-Fives** (1.5 minutes)

Setup:
1. Face each other in push-up position
2. About 2 feet apart

Action:
- Both do a push-up
- At the top, high-five with right hands
- Another push-up
- High-five with left hands
- 10 push-ups each

*Works: Chest, core, coordination*

---

**Exercise 5: Plank Hand Slaps** (1.5 minutes)

Setup:
1. Plank position facing each other
2. Close enough to reach partner's hands

Action:
- Try to tap partner's hands
- Partner tries to avoid while staying in plank
- Like a game!
- 30 seconds each as "attacker"

*Works: Core stability, shoulders, fun!*

---

**Rest 30 seconds**

## Lower Body Partners (7 minutes)

### Legs and Glutes

**Exercise 1: Wheelbarrow Walks** (1.5 minutes)

Setup:
1. A in push-up position
2. B holds A's ankles

Action:
- A walks forward on hands
- B walks behind holding ankles
- 20 steps forward
- Switch roles

*Works: Arms (walker), grip (holder), core (both)*

---

**Exercise 2: Partner Wall Sit** (1.5 minutes)

Setup:
1. Stand back to back
2. No wall needed - you ARE the wall!

Action:
- Slowly walk feet out together
- Lower into squat position
- Support each other's backs
- Hold as long as possible!
- 45 seconds goal

*Works: Quads, communication*

---

**Exercise 3: Resistance Leg Press** (1.5 minutes)

Setup:
1. A lies on back, knees bent, feet up
2. B places hands on A's feet

Action:
- A presses feet forward
- B provides resistance
- Like a leg press machine!
- 10 reps, then switch

*Works: Quads, glutes*

---

**Exercise 4: Partner Squat Jump Challenge** (1 minute)

Setup:
1. Face each other holding hands

Action:
- Squat down together
- Jump up together
- Land soft, squat immediately
- 10 synchronized jump squats

*Works: Legs, cardio, coordination*

---

**Exercise 5: Single Leg Balance Challenge** (1.5 minutes)

Setup:
1. Face each other on one leg
2. Hold hands for support

Action:
- Both balance on same leg (right)
- Try to make each other wobble (gently!)
- Stay balanced!
- 30 seconds each leg

*Works: Balance, stability, ankles*

---

**Rest 30 seconds**

## Core Partners (5 minutes)

### Abs and Stability

**Exercise 1: Partner Sit-Up Passes** (1.5 minutes)

Setup:
1. Lie head-to-head, about 1 foot apart
2. Or face each other, feet interlocked

Action:
- Both do sit-ups
- At the top, pass a ball (or pretend!)
- Back down, back up, receive
- 20 sit-ups

*Works: Abs, coordination*

---

**Exercise 2: Plank Partner Push** (1 minute)

Setup:
1. Both in plank position, side by side

Action:
- Try to gently push partner off balance
- Shoulder bumps allowed
- Stay in plank!
- 30 seconds, switch sides

*Works: Core stability, fun*

---

**Exercise 3: Leg Throw-Downs** (1.5 minutes)

Setup:
1. A lies on back, holding B's ankles
2. B stands at A's head

Action:
- A raises legs to B
- B pushes legs down (various directions!)
- A must control the lower
- 10 reps, then switch

*Works: Lower abs intensely!*

---

**Exercise 4: Partner Bicycle** (1 minute)

Setup:
1. Lie on backs, feet touching
2. Legs up in the air

Action:
- "Pedal" together
- Feet pressing against each other
- Forward and backward
- It's like tandem biking!

*Works: Abs, hip flexors*

## Partner Challenge Games (3 minutes)

### Make It Fun!

**Wheelbarrow Race** (1 minute)
- Set a course
- Race other pairs or against time
- Switch positions halfway!

**Plank Stare Down** (1 minute)
- Both in plank
- First to drop loses
- No laughing! (hard not to)

**Synchronized Burpee Challenge** (1 minute)
- Do burpees in sync
- If you get out of sync, start count over
- How many perfectly synced burpees?

## Cool-Down: Partner Stretches (3 minutes)

### Stretch Together

**Seated Forward Fold Assist** (45 seconds each)
- A sits with legs extended
- B gently presses A's back
- Gentle increase in stretch
- Switch roles

**Back-to-Back Twist Stretch** (45 seconds)
- Sit back to back
- Both twist right, hold hands
- Hold the stretch
- Switch to left

**Partner Quad Stretch** (30 seconds each leg)
- A stands on one leg
- B holds A's ankle (quad stretch)
- Switch legs, switch roles

**Final Partner Stretch** (30 seconds)
- Face each other
- Hold hands, lean back
- Full body stretch!
- Thank your partner!

## Partner Communication Cues

### During Exercises

| What to Say | When |
|-------------|------|
| "More resistance" | Need more challenge |
| "Less pressure" | Too hard |
| "Hold there" | Perfect resistance |
| "Ready?" | Before starting |
| "Switching!" | Changing roles |
| "Break!" | Need to stop |

### Encouragement
- "You've got this!"
- "Almost there!"
- "Great form!"
- "One more!"
- "We're doing it!"

## Pairing Modifications

### Adult + Child
- Adult provides controlled resistance
- Child can "push" adult with full effort
- Make it feel successful for the child
- Celebrate the child's strength

### Size Differences
- Larger partner uses less force
- Adjust positions as needed
- Focus on the movement, not the competition
- Everyone can get a workout!

### Different Fitness Levels
- More fit partner adjusts resistance
- Take turns leading
- No judgment
- Growth mindset!

## Partner Workout Benefits

### Physical
- True resistance training
- Adjustable intensity
- Full body workout
- No equipment needed

### Relationship
- Trust building
- Communication practice
- Shared accomplishment
- Quality time together

### Mental
- Accountability
- More motivation
- Less likely to quit
- Fun factor!
