## Welcome to the Animal Kingdom!

Today we're going on a yoga safari where we'll transform our bodies into amazing animals! This practice is perfect for families with young children, making yoga playful and memorable.

## Why Animal Yoga Works for Kids

- **Imagination**: Stories engage young minds
- **Memorable**: Animal names make poses stick
- **Playful**: Yoga becomes a game, not exercise
- **Flexible timing**: Hold poses as long as interest lasts
- **Builds vocabulary**: Learn about animals worldwide

## Setting Up Your Safari

1. Clear a soft space (carpet, grass, or yoga mats)
2. Wear comfortable clothes
3. No shoes needed
4. Have water nearby
5. Bring stuffed animals to join the adventure!

## Our Animal Yoga Journey

### Opening: Wake Up in the Jungle! (2 minutes)

**Morning Stretch**
- Sit cross-legged
- Reach arms high: "Good morning, sun!"
- Twist right: "Hello, trees!"
- Twist left: "Hello, flowers!"
- Forward fold: "Hello, earth!"

### Safari Animals (15 minutes)

**ü¶Å Lion Pose (Simhasana)**
"Let's become brave lions!"
1. Kneel on mat, sit back on heels
2. Hands on knees, fingers spread like claws
3. Breathe in deeply
4. Exhale: Open mouth wide, stick tongue out
5. ROAR! Make the biggest lion sound!
6. Great for: Releasing energy, opening throat

**üê± Cat and Cow**
"Now we're barn cats waking up!"
1. Hands and knees on mat
2. COW: Drop belly, look up, "Moo!"
3. CAT: Round back up, tuck chin, "Meow!"
4. Flow between them slowly
5. 5-8 rounds
6. Great for: Spine flexibility, coordination

**üêï Downward Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)**
"Stretch like a happy puppy!"
1. From hands and knees, lift hips up
2. Make an upside-down V shape
3. Pedal feet (walk the dog!)
4. Wag your tail (wiggle hips)
5. Hold 5-10 breaths
6. Great for: Full body stretch, builds strength

**üêç Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana)**
"Slither like a snake!"
1. Lie on belly
2. Hands under shoulders
3. Press up, straighten arms partially
4. Look up, hiss: "Ssssssss"
5. Wiggle side to side like a snake
6. Great for: Back strength, chest opening

**üê∏ Frog Pose**
"Hop to your lily pad!"
1. Squat with feet slightly apart
2. Hands between feet (or on floor in front)
3. Bounce gently
4. Hop! (if energy allows)
5. "Ribbit, ribbit!"
6. Great for: Hip opening, leg strength

**ü¶ã Butterfly Pose (Baddha Konasana)**
"Beautiful butterflies resting on flowers!"
1. Sit with soles of feet together
2. Hold ankles or feet
3. Gently flutter knees up and down
4. Pretend wings are drying in sun
5. Hold 30 seconds
6. Great for: Hip flexibility, calming

**üêª Bear Walk**
"Bears walking through the forest!"
1. Hands and feet on ground (not knees)
2. Walk forward on all fours
3. Right hand + right foot together
4. Left hand + left foot together
5. Walk around the room
6. Great for: Coordination, full body

**ü¶© Flamingo Pose (Tree Pose for Kids)**
"Balance like a flamingo!"
1. Stand tall on one leg
2. Other foot on ankle, calf, or thigh (not knee!)
3. Arms can be out for balance, or overhead
4. Find a spot to focus eyes on
5. Switch legs
6. Hold 10-30 seconds each side
7. Great for: Balance, focus

**üê¢ Turtle Pose**
"Hide in your shell!"
1. Sit with legs wide
2. Slide arms under knees
3. Round forward slowly
4. Tuck head down (hiding!)
5. Stay 5-8 breaths
6. Pop head out: "Hello!"
7. Great for: Hip stretch, calming

### Ocean Animals (Optional Extension)

**üê† Fish Pose**
"Swimming through the sea!"
1. Lie on back
2. Slide hands under bottom
3. Press elbows down, lift chest
4. Gently drop head back
5. Breathe like you're under water
6. Great for: Chest opening, breathing

**ü¶≠ Seal Pose**
"Sunbathing seals!"
1. Lie on belly
2. Press up on hands (arms straight)
3. Tilt head back
4. Make seal sounds: "Arf arf!"
5. Clap feet together
6. Great for: Back flexibility

**üêô Octopus (Happy Baby)**
"Wiggly octopus tentacles!"
1. Lie on back
2. Hold feet from outside
3. Gently rock side to side
4. Wiggle legs like tentacles!
5. Great for: Hip release, playfulness

### Rest: Sleeping Animals (3 minutes)

**üê® Sleepy Koala (Savasana)**
"Time for all animals to rest..."
1. Lie on back, arms by sides
2. Close eyes
3. Imagine you're a sleepy koala in a tree
4. Breathe slowly
5. Feel your body sink into the ground
6. Rest for 2-3 minutes

**Gentle Awakening**
- Wiggle fingers and toes
- Stretch arms overhead
- Hug knees to chest
- Roll to one side
- Slowly sit up
- Gentle namaste: "Thank you, animal friends!"

## Tips for Parents

### Making It Work for Different Ages

**Toddlers (2-3)**
- Keep it super short (5-10 minutes)
- Focus on 3-4 animals only
- Lots of sound effects
- Let them lead sometimes
- Don't worry about "correct" poses

**Preschool (3-5)**
- Can follow longer sequences
- Love adding details: "What color is your butterfly?"
- May want to repeat favorites
- Great with animal music/sounds playing

**Kids (6-8)**
- Can learn Sanskrit names (they think they're cool!)
- Hold poses longer
- Add breathing techniques
- Can help teach younger siblings

### Dealing with Wiggles

- Wiggles are okay! Young children need movement
- Add transitions: "Hop like a frog to your mat!"
- If attention wanders, move to next animal
- End early rather than force it

## Make It Your Own

**Create an Animal Story**
String poses into a narrative:
"The frog hopped to the pond where he met a turtle. They saw a butterfly fly overhead and a snake slither by..."

**Animal Matching Game**
- Say an animal
- Everyone makes up a pose for that animal
- No wrong answers!

**Habitat Hunt**
