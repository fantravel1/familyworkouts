# Bedtime Stretches for the Whole Family

Create a peaceful transition from the busy day to restful sleep. These gentle stretches help release tension while the breathing exercises calm busy minds.

## Why Bedtime Stretching?

- **Signals sleep time**: Body learns routine
- **Releases tension**: Melts away the day's stress
- **Calms the mind**: Breathing slows racing thoughts
- **Family bonding**: Quiet connection time
- **Better sleep**: Proven to improve sleep quality

## Setting the Scene

### Create a Calm Environment

- Dim the lights
- Turn off screens
- Quiet background music (optional)
- Comfortable clothes
- Room temperature comfortable
- Maybe light a (safe) candle

## Opening Breathing (2 minutes)

### Finding Your Breath

**Belly Breathing** (1 minute)

*How to do it:*
1. Lie on your back or sit comfortably
2. Place hands on belly
3. Breathe in through noseâ€”belly rises
4. Breathe out through mouthâ€”belly falls
5. Feel hands rise and fall
6. 6-8 slow breaths

*For little ones:*
- Put a stuffed animal on their belly
- "Make your stuffy go for a ride!"
- Watch it rise and fall

**4-4-4 Breathing** (1 minute)

1. Breathe IN for 4 counts
2. HOLD for 4 counts
3. Breathe OUT for 4 counts
4. Repeat 4 times

*Count together as a family*

## Gentle Stretches: Upper Body (4 minutes)

### Neck Stretches (1 minute)

**Ear to Shoulder**
1. Sit or stand comfortably
2. Gently drop right ear toward right shoulder
3. Hold for 5 slow breaths
4. Return to center
5. Repeat on left side

**Chin Drops**
1. Let chin drop toward chest
2. Feel gentle stretch in neck back
3. Hold for 5 slow breaths
4. Slowly roll head side to side

### Shoulder Release (1 minute)

**Shoulder Rolls**
1. Roll shoulders up to ears
2. Roll them back
3. Drop them down
4. Repeat 5 times slowly
5. Feel tension melting away

**Squeeze and Release**
1. Squeeze shoulders up tight
2. Hold for 5 seconds
3. Release and let them drop
4. Feel the difference!
5. Repeat 3 times

### Arm Stretches (1 minute)

**Cross-Body Stretch**
1. Extend right arm across body
2. Left hand gently pulls at elbow
3. Hold for 5 breaths
4. Switch arms

**Overhead Reach**
1. Interlace fingers
2. Reach arms overhead
3. Stretch up and slightly back
4. Hold for 5 breaths
5. Release with a sigh

### Side Stretches (1 minute)

**Seated Side Bend**
1. Sit cross-legged (or any comfortable seated position)
2. Right hand on floor beside you
3. Left arm reaches up and over
4. Feel stretch along left side
5. Hold for 5 breaths
6. Switch sides

## Gentle Stretches: Lower Body (4 minutes)

### Hip Openers (1.5 minutes)

**Butterfly Pose**
1. Sit with soles of feet together
2. Knees fall open like butterfly wings
3. Hold feet with hands
4. Gentle flap wings up and down
5. Then hold still, breathe deeply
6. Optional: Lean forward slightly

**Happy Baby**
1. Lie on your back
2. Bring knees toward armpits
3. Hold outside edges of feet
4. Gently rock side to side
5. "Happy babies rock to sleep!"

### Leg Stretches (1.5 minutes)

**Lying Hamstring Stretch**
1. Lie on your back
2. Hug right knee to chest
3. Straighten leg toward ceiling
4. Use hands behind thigh for support
5. Hold for 5 breaths
6. Switch legs

**Lying Twist**
1. Lie on back, arms in T position
2. Hug knees to chest
3. Drop knees to right side
4. Turn head to left
5. Hold for 5 breaths
6. Switch sides

### Back Release (1 minute)

**Cat-Cow Stretch**
1. Hands and knees position
2. INHALE: Drop belly, look up (cow)
3. EXHALE: Round back, tuck chin (cat)
4. Move slowly with breath
5. 5-6 rounds

**Child's Pose**
1. Knees wide, big toes touching
2. Sit hips back toward heels
3. Arms extended forward
4. Forehead rests on floor
5. Breathe into your back
6. Hold for 30 seconds

## Family Calm Down Sequence (3 minutes)

### Progressive Relaxation

*Do this lying down, speaking softly*

**Script to Read:**

"Let's relax our bodies from toes to head...

**Feet** - Squeeze your toes tight, tight, tight... and release. Feel your feet relax.

**Legs** - Squeeze your legs tight like you're standing on tiptoes... and release. Feel your legs sink into the floor.

**Belly** - Make your tummy tight like someone might tickle you... and release. Feel your belly soften.

**Hands** - Make tight fists, squeeze, squeeze... and release. Feel your hands go floppy.

**Arms** - Make your arms super stiff like a robot... and release. Feel your arms get heavy.

**Shoulders** - Squeeze shoulders up to your ears... and release. Feel them melt down.

**Face** - Scrunch your whole face tight... and release. Feel your face smooth and calm.

**Whole body** - One more time, squeeze EVERYTHING tight... and releeeease. Your whole body is calm and relaxed."

## Closing Ritual (2 minutes)

### Gratitude Moment

*Each family member shares:*
- One thing that made them happy today
- One thing they're grateful for
- One good thing about tomorrow

### Good Night Wishes

**Options:**
- "Sleep well, see you in the morning"
- Butterfly kisses
- Forehead kisses
- Special family phrase
- Group hug

### Final Breathing

**5 Deep Breaths Together**
1. Deep breath in... slow breath out
2. Deep breath in... slow breath out
3. Deep breath in... slow breath out
4. Deep breath in... slow breath out
5. Deep breath in... and let it all go

*"Good night, bodies. Good night, minds."*

## Age Modifications

### Toddlers (2-4)
- Shorter routine (5-7 minutes)
- Simple movements
- Use stuffed animals
- Lots of imagery ("melt like ice cream")
- Parent guides the whole time

### Little Kids (5-8)
- Full routine with simpler instructions
- Include favorite stretches
- They can lead one stretch
- Fun names for poses
- Stories during relaxation

### Older Kids & Teens (9+)
- Full routine independently
- Can add more stretches
- Deeper breathing exercises
- Journal option after
- Solo practice okay

### Adults
- Add any additional stretches needed
- Lead the family routine
- Model calm energy
- Use this time to decompress too

## Making It a Habit

### Tips for Consistency

1. **Same time**: Right before brushing teeth
2. **Same place**: Bedroom or living room floor
3. **Same order**: Predictability helps
4. **Non-negotiable**: Part of bedtime routine
5. **Keep it short**: 10-15 minutes max

### If Time Is Short

**5-Minute Version:**
- 1 minute breathing
- Neck stretches
- Butterfly pose
- Child's pose
- Progressive relaxation (quick)

**3-Minute Version:**
- 30 seconds breathing
- One stretch
- Lying down relaxation
- Good night

### If Someone Resists

- Don't force it
- Invite, don't require
- They can just watch
- Make it optional initially
- Let positive peer pressure work
- Find what they DO enjoy

## Tracking Sleep Improvement

### Simple Sleep Log

Rate each morning (1-5 scale):
- How quickly fell asleep?
- How well slept through night?
- How rested in morning?

After 2 weeks, look for patterns!

### Signs It's Working

- Falling asleep faster
- Fewer night wakings
- Better morning mood
- Kids look forward to it
- Calmer bedtime overall
