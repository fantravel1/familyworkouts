## Better Together

Partner yoga transforms individual practice into shared experience. You'll support each other, communicate constantly, and create beautiful poses neither could do alone. Plus, it's surprisingly fun!

## Why Partner Yoga is Special

- **Trust building**: Literally supporting each other
- **Communication**: Must talk through each pose
- **Physical connection**: Safe, appropriate touch
- **Shared achievement**: Celebrate together
- **Relationship strengthening**: Quality time embodied

## Partner Pairings

Great combinations:
- Parent + child
- Parent + teen
- Siblings together
- Grandparent + grandchild
- Two adults
- Switch partners throughout!

## Guidelines for Practice

1. **Communicate constantly**: "Ready?" "More?" "Less?"
2. **Start gentle**: You can always go deeper
3. **No forcing**: If it doesn't work, try differently
4. **Laugh freely**: It's supposed to be fun!
5. **Support, don't compete**: You're a team

## Warm-Up: Connect (4 minutes)

### Back-to-Back Breathing
1. Sit back-to-back with partner
2. Both cross legs comfortably
3. Feel each other's backs
4. Breathe together
5. Try to sync your breaths
6. 1 minute

*"Feel your partner's breath against your back"*

### Back-to-Back Stand Up
1. Stay back-to-back
2. Link arms at elbows
3. Press backs together
4. Walk feet in
5. Push against each other to stand up!
6. Reverse to sit down
7. Try 3 times

*"Teamwork makes it work!"*

### Seated Twist Together
1. Sit back-to-back
2. Both twist right
3. Right hand to your left knee
4. Left hand reaches back to partner's right knee
5. Hold 5 breaths
6. Switch directions

*"Twist and reach for connection"*

## Partner Poses (15 minutes)

### 1. Double Tree Pose üå≥üå≥
*Two trees growing together*

**Setup:**
1. Stand side by side, hips touching
2. Inside arms around each other's waist
3. Outside arms reach up, palms touch overhead

**The Pose:**
1. Both lift outside leg into tree pose
2. Foot on ankle, calf, or thigh (not knee!)
3. Find balance together
4. Hold 5-8 breaths
5. Switch sides

**Tips:**
- Start with both feet down
- Add leg lift when stable
- Use each other for balance!

---

### 2. Partner Forward Fold
*Gentle stretch with assistance*

**Setup:**
1. Sit facing each other
2. Legs extended, feet touch
3. Hold hands or wrists

**The Pose:**
1. One person folds forward
2. Other person gently leans back
3. Creates deeper stretch for the folder
4. Hold 5 breaths
5. Switch roles

**Tips:**
- Communicate! "More?" "Good there?"
- Never pull too hard
- Folder relaxes into it

---

### 3. Double Boat Pose ‚õµ‚õµ
*Sailing together!*

**Setup:**
1. Sit facing each other, knees bent
2. Hold hands or wrists
3. Soles of feet press together

**The Pose:**
1. Slowly straighten legs upward
2. Balance on sit bones
3. Create a "W" shape with legs
4. Keep holding hands
5. Hold 5-8 breaths (or longer!)

**Tips:**
- Bent knees version first
- One leg at a time
- Core engaged!
- It's okay to wobble

---

### 4. Partner Seated Twist
*Deeper twist with help*

**Setup:**
1. Sit back-to-back
2. Legs crossed or extended

**The Pose:**
1. Both twist to the right
2. Reach right hand back to partner's left knee
3. Left hand to your own right knee
4. Partner's hands support your twist
5. Hold 5 breaths
6. Switch directions

**Tips:**
- Gentle twist‚Äîlet partner's touch guide
- Sit up tall throughout
- Breathe into the twist

---

### 5. Double Downward Dog üêïüêï
*Stacked dogs!*

**Setup:**
1. Person A: Regular downward dog
2. Person B: Stands at A's hips

**The Pose:**
1. Person B places hands in front of A's hands
2. Person B walks feet onto A's lower back
3. Person B presses into downward dog on top!
4. Hold 5 breaths
5. Carefully come down
6. Switch (if appropriate sizes)

**Tips:**
- Bottom person must be stable and strong
- Top person should be lighter (often child)
- Go slowly!
- Skip if sizes don't work‚Äîthat's okay!

---

### 6. Flying Superhero (Airplane)
*The ultimate trust pose!*

**Setup:**
1. Base (stronger partner) lies on back
2. Flyer stands at base's feet

**The Pose:**
1. Base bends knees, feet on flyer's hips
2. Flyer leans forward, holds base's hands
3. Base straightens legs, lifting flyer
4. Flyer extends arms like flying!
5. Hold 5-10 breaths
6. Come down slowly

**Tips:**
- Base: Keep legs vertical, not angled
- Flyer: Keep body straight and strong
- Both: COMMUNICATE!
- Start small, build up

---

### 7. Partner Warrior III
*Flying together!*

**Setup:**
1. Stand facing each other, arm's length apart
2. Hold each other's shoulders or hands

**The Pose:**
1. Both lean forward
2. Both lift same leg behind (e.g., right)
3. Create a counterbalance
4. Bodies form "T" shapes
5. Hold 5 breaths
6. Switch legs

**Tips:**
- Push gently into each other's hands
- Look at your partner's eyes
- Same leg at same time!

---

### 8. Heart-to-Heart Relaxation üíï
*Ending with connection*

**Setup:**
1. Both lie on backs
2. Position so heads are near each other
3. Arms overhead to hold hands

**The Pose:**
1. Just rest and breathe
2. Feel connected through hands
3. Sync breathing if possible
4. Rest 2-3 minutes

*"Thank you for practicing with me"*

## Troubleshooting

**"We keep falling!"**
- Lower your expectations
- Falling = learning
- Laugh and try again
- Use a wall for support

**"My partner is too heavy/light"**
- Choose appropriate poses
- Some work better for matched sizes
- Flying poses need size difference

**"We can't stop laughing"**
- GOOD! That's part of it!
- Laughter is yoga too
- Come back to breath, try again

**"This feels awkward"**
- Start with less intimate poses
- Build comfort over time
- It's okay to take breaks

## Partner Yoga for Different Pairs

### Parent + Young Child (3-6)
- Keep it playful
- Flying poses are usually hits
- Let them "win" sometimes
- Short attention span is normal

### Parent + Older Child (7-12)
- Can do most poses
- Give them leadership roles
- Celebrate their strength
- Real bonding opportunity

### Parent + Teen
- They may act reluctant
- Don't force it
- When they engage, it's golden
- Respect their boundaries

### Adult Partners
- Full expression of all poses
- Great for reconnection
- Can add more challenge
- Consider candlelight!

### Grandparent + Grandchild
- Modify for comfort
- Focus on seated poses
- Gentle, precious connection
- Memory-making!
