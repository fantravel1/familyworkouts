## Setting Up Your Rainbow Space

1. Find a quiet, comfortable spot
2. Lie on your back on a mat or soft surface
3. Optional: Cover with a light blanket
4. Dim the lights if possible
5. Take three deep breaths to begin

## Family Rainbow Activities

### Rainbow Breathing Cards
- Make 7 cards, one per color
- Draw or glue colored paper
- Practice the breath for each color
- Pull a card when you need calm

### Rainbow Jar
- Fill a jar with water
- Add glitter or food coloring (one color per day)
- Shake it and watch colors settle
- Practice stillness as colors settle

### Rainbow Walk
- Go outside and find one thing of each color
- At each discovery, do that color's breath
- Find your rainbow in nature!

## Tips for Parents

### Leading the Practice
- Read slowly, with pauses
- Your calm voice sets the tone
- It's okay if kids wiggle
- Adapt language for age

### For Younger Children
- Use just 3-4 colors
- Make movements bigger/more active
- Use stuffed animal as breathing buddy
- Keep it playful

### For Older Children
- Introduce chakra concepts (age-appropriate)
- Connect to emotional awareness
- Journal about colors and feelings
- Let them lead themselves

### For Teens
- Position as stress management tool
- Connect to mindfulness science
- Use during homework breaks
- Respect if they prefer privacy

## The Science Behind Colors

**Fun facts to share:**

- Colors can affect our mood (chromotherapy)
- Red can energize, blue can calm
- Green is restful for our eyes
- Each color has a different wavelength
- Our brains respond to colors even with eyes closed!
