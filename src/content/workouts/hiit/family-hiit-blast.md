## What is HIIT?

High-Intensity Interval Training alternates between intense bursts of activity and fixed periods of rest. It's incredibly efficientâ€”you get maximum results in minimum time, perfect for busy families!

## Why HIIT Works for Families

- **Time-efficient**: 20 minutes delivers serious results
- **Scalable**: Everyone works at THEIR maximum effort
- **Fun**: Game-like structure with clear intervals
- **No equipment**: Bodyweight only
- **Energy release**: Perfect for active kids

## The Rules

1. **Work Hard During Work**: Give it your all
2. **Rest During Rest**: Actually restâ€”you'll need it
3. **No Competition**: Your 100% is different from everyone else's
4. **Modifications Welcome**: Any movement counts
5. **Have Fun**: Cheer each other on!

## Today's Workout Structure

**Format**: 30 seconds work / 15 seconds rest
**Rounds**: 3 rounds of 6 exercises
**Total Time**: ~20 minutes (including warm-up and cool-down)

---

## Warm-Up (3 minutes)

**March in Place** - 30 seconds
- Pump those arms!

**Arm Circles** - 30 seconds
- Big circles forward, then backward

**Hip Circles** - 30 seconds
- Hands on hips, circle those hips

**Leg Swings** - 30 seconds
- Hold wall, swing each leg front to back

**Jumping Jacks** - 30 seconds (half speed)
- Easy pace to elevate heart rate

**High Knees** - 30 seconds (half speed)
- Gradual increase in intensity

---

## Main Workout: 3 Rounds

### Exercise 1: Jump Squats (or Squats)
**30 seconds work / 15 seconds rest**

*Full version:*
- Squat down, explode up into a jump
- Land softly, immediately squat again
- Keep chest up throughout

*Modification for kids/beginners:*
- Regular squats without the jump
- Or small hops instead of full jumps

*Modification for seniors:*
- Chair-assisted squats
- Or wall sit hold

### Exercise 2: Mountain Climbers
**30 seconds work / 15 seconds rest**

*Full version:*
- Plank position
- Drive knees to chest alternating
- Keep hips down, core tight

*Modification:*
- Slower pace
- Hands on elevated surface (step, couch)
- Standing knee drives

### Exercise 3: Burpees (or Squat Thrusts)
**30 seconds work / 15 seconds rest**

*Full version:*
- Stand, drop to squat, hands down
- Jump feet back to plank
- Jump feet forward
- Explode up with jump
- Repeat!

*Modification for kids:*
- No push-up, no jump at top
- "Squat thrust" version

*Modification for beginners:*
- Step back instead of jump
- Stand up without jump

### Exercise 4: Plank Shoulder Taps
**30 seconds work / 15 seconds rest**

*Full version:*
- Plank position, feet wide for stability
- Tap left shoulder with right hand
- Tap right shoulder with left hand
- Keep hips still (no rocking!)

*Modification:*
- Knees down
- Regular plank hold
- Wall plank with taps

### Exercise 5: Speed Skaters
**30 seconds work / 15 seconds rest**

*Full version:*
- Leap side to side
- Land on one foot, other leg sweeps behind
- Touch floor with opposite hand
- Explode to other side

*Modification:*
- Step instead of leap
- Smaller side-to-side movement
- No floor touch

### Exercise 6: High Knees
**30 seconds work / 15 seconds rest**

*Full version:*
- Run in place
- Drive knees high (hip level)
- Pump arms
- Stay on balls of feet

*Modification:*
- Marching (one foot always down)
- Slower pace
- Seated knee drives

---

**After each round:**
- 30 seconds complete rest
- Drink water
- High-fives all around!
- Then start round 2 (and 3!)

---

## Cool-Down (3 minutes)

**Walking in Place** - 30 seconds
- Slow it down, catch breath

**Standing Quad Stretch** - 30 seconds each leg
- Hold wall for balance

**Standing Hamstring Stretch** - 30 seconds each leg
- Foot on low surface, lean forward

**Chest Opener** - 30 seconds
- Clasp hands behind back, open chest

**Child's Pose** - 30 seconds
- Knees wide, reach arms forward

**Deep Breathing** - 30 seconds
- In through nose, out through mouth
- Celebrate completing the workout!

## Family Challenge Variations

### "Follow the Leader"
- Each family member picks one exercise
- Everyone does 30 seconds of each person's choice
- Get creative!

### "Dice Workout"
- Roll a die (or use random number generator)
- Number = which exercise Ã— 10 reps
- Take turns rolling

### "Beat Your Score"
- Count total reps of one exercise
- Try to beat family's combined score next week

---

## Age-Specific Tips

### Kids (6-12)
- Focus on fun over form
- Shorter work periods if needed (20 seconds)
- Extra rest between rounds
- Let them pick music!
- Lots of encouragement

### Teens
- Challenge with full versions
- Track personal records
- Make it social: Invite friends
- Add exercise variations

### Adults
- Model proper form
- Don't sandbagâ€”kids notice!
- Focus on your workout while supervising
- Great stress relief

### Seniors (with clearance from doctor)
- All modifications are perfect
- Chair-based versions of everything
- Focus on movement over intensity
- Longer rest periods
- Listen to your body

---

## Safety First

1. **Clear the area** - No obstacles, no pets underfoot
2. **Proper footwear** - Supportive shoes (or safe bare feet)
3. **Hydrate** - Water before, during, after
4. **Listen to your body** - Rest if you need to
5. **Medical clearance** - Check with doctor if new to exercise
