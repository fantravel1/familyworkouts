## Dynamic Warm-Up (4 minutes)

### Get Your Body Ready

**Arm Circles** (30 seconds)
- Small circles â†’ big circles
- Forward then backward
- Shoulders loose and warm

**Leg Swings** (30 seconds each leg)
- Hold wall or chair
- Swing leg front to back
- Control the swing
- Dynamic hip stretch

**Torso Twists** (30 seconds)
- Feet planted
- Twist left, twist right
- Arms follow naturally
- Wake up the core

**High Knees** (30 seconds)
- Drive knees up
- Quick pace
- Arms pumping
- Heart rate rising

**Butt Kicks** (30 seconds)
- Kick heels to glutes
- Quick feet
- Arms moving
- Warming up hamstrings

**Jumping Jacks** (30 seconds)
- Full extension
- Controlled landing
- Classic warm-up
- Getting pumped!

**Inchworms** (30 seconds)
- Walk hands out to plank
- Walk feet to hands
- Full body activation
- 4-5 reps

## Block 1: Lower Body Blast (5 minutes)

### Build That Foundation

**Squat Pulses** (45 seconds)
1. Squat to 90 degrees
2. Pulse up and down (small movements)
3. Stay in the squat the whole time
4. Burn is real!
5. 30 seconds rest

**Jump Squats** (45 seconds)
1. Squat down
2. Explode up
3. Land soft, absorb impact
4. Right back into squat
5. 30 seconds rest

**Alternating Lunges** (45 seconds)
1. Step forward into lunge
2. Push back to standing
3. Other leg forward
4. Keep chest up
5. 30 seconds rest

**Wall Sit** (45 seconds)
1. Back against wall
2. Thighs parallel to floor
3. Hold position
4. Legs will shake - that's good!
5. 30 seconds rest

## Block 2: Core Crusher (4 minutes)

### Strong Core = Strong Everything

**Plank Hold** (45 seconds)
1. Forearms on ground
2. Body straight as a board
3. No sagging hips!
4. No pike up!
5. Hold strong
6. 15 seconds rest

**Mountain Climbers** (45 seconds)
1. Plank position
2. Drive knees to chest alternating
3. Fast feet!
4. Core engaged
5. 15 seconds rest

**Bicycle Crunches** (45 seconds)
1. Lie on back
2. Hands behind head
3. Elbow to opposite knee
4. Alternate sides
5. Control the movement
6. 15 seconds rest

**Plank Shoulder Taps** (45 seconds)
1. High plank (hands not forearms)
2. Touch left shoulder with right hand
3. Touch right shoulder with left hand
4. Keep hips STILL
5. Core works hard!
6. 15 seconds rest

## Block 3: Upper Body Push (4 minutes)

### Real Strength

**Push-Ups** (45 seconds)
1. Choose your level:
   - Knees down (building up)
   - Full push-ups
   - Diamond push-ups (advanced)
2. Chest to floor
3. Push up strong
4. 15 seconds rest

**Tricep Dips** (45 seconds)
1. Hands on chair/step behind you
2. Lower body down
3. Push up using triceps
4. Keep elbows pointing back
5. 15 seconds rest

**Push-Up Hold** (30 seconds)
1. Lower halfway down
2. HOLD that position
3. Shake is normal
4. Building strength!
5. 15 seconds rest

**Arm Circles (Weighted Feel)** (30 seconds)
1. Arms straight out
2. Small circles forward
3. Keep going...
4. It burns!
5. Reverse direction!

## Block 4: HIIT Finisher (5 minutes)

### Leave It All Here

**Circuit: 40 seconds work / 20 seconds rest**

**Round 1: Burpees**
1. Drop to floor
2. Push-up
3. Jump feet in
4. Jump up with arms overhead
5. Repeat for 40 seconds
6. 20 seconds rest

**Round 2: Speed Skaters**
1. Leap side to side
2. Land on one foot
3. Other leg crosses behind
4. Athletic movement
5. 40 seconds work
6. 20 seconds rest

**Round 3: High Knees Sprint**
1. Run in place
2. Knees HIGH
3. Maximum speed
4. Arms pumping
5. 40 seconds work
6. 20 seconds rest

**Round 4: Squat Jumps**
1. Squat deep
2. Explode up
3. Soft landing
4. Maximum height
5. 40 seconds work
6. 20 seconds rest

**Round 5: Power Push-Ups**
1. Push-up with explosive push
2. Hands leave ground if possible
3. Control landing
4. Modified: Fast regular push-ups
5. 40 seconds work
6. Done!

## Cool-Down (3 minutes)

### Recovery Matters

**Standing Quad Stretch** (30 seconds each leg)
- Pull foot to glutes
- Balance on one leg
- Hold and breathe

**Hamstring Stretch** (30 seconds each leg)
- Foot on raised surface or straight ahead
- Bend forward at hips
- Feel the stretch

**Hip Flexor Lunge** (30 seconds each side)
- Lunge position
- Push hips forward
- Arms up if comfortable

**Chest Opener** (20 seconds)
- Clasp hands behind back
- Open chest, squeeze shoulders
- Deep breath

**Shoulder Stretch** (20 seconds each arm)
- Arm across body
- Pull at elbow
- Both sides

**Deep Breathing** (30 seconds)
- Stand tall
- 5 deep breaths
- Recovery begins

## Tween Fitness Facts

### Your Body Now
- Growth plates are developing - avoid SUPER heavy weights
- Bodyweight training is perfect for this age
- Flexibility may decrease (stretch regularly!)
- Coordination is improving rapidly
- You can build real strength

### What Matters
- **Consistency**: 3-4 times per week beats random hard workouts
- **Form**: Do it right or don't do it
- **Sleep**: 8-10 hours helps you recover and grow
- **Nutrition**: Fuel your body with good food
- **Balance**: Sports, fitness, rest, fun

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### âŒ Don't:
- Skip warm-up
- Sacrifice form for reps
- Compare yourself to adults
- Work out through pain
- Forget to cool down

### âœ… Do:
- Warm up properly
- Focus on good form
- Track YOUR progress
- Stop if something hurts

## Motivation Tips

### When You Don't Feel Like It
- Start with just 5 minutes (you'll probably keep going)
- Put on your favorite music
- Work out with family or friends
- Remember your goals
- Some workout > no workout

### Setting Goals
- "I want to do 10 real push-ups"
- "I want to run a faster mile"
- "I want to make the team"
- Write them down!
- Celebrate when you hit them!

## Parent Note

### Supporting Your Tween
- Let them have ownership of their fitness
- Offer to work out together (if they want!)
- Praise effort, not just results
- Don't criticize their body
- Make equipment available if needed
- Respect when they want space

### Signs of Overtraining
- Excessive fatigue
- Mood changes
- Declining performance
- Frequent illness
- Sleep problems
- Loss of motivation

**Balance is key!**
