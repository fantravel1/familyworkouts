### Warm-Up (5 minutes)

**Head Circles**
- Slow circles right x 5
- Slow circles left x 5
- Never rush neck movements!

**Shoulder Rolls**
- Big rolls backward x 10
- Big rolls forward x 10

**Waist Rotations**
- Hands on hips
- Circle hips/waist
- 10 each direction

**Leg Swings**
- Hold wall for balance
- Swing leg forward/back x 10
- Swing leg side to side x 10

**Toe Touches**
- Legs together
- Reach for toes
- Hold 20 seconds
- Wushu requires flexibility!

### Form Practice: Basic Sequence (3 minutes)

**"Dragon Awakens" Mini Form:**

Starting position: Ready stance with salute

1. Step left into bow stance, left palm push
2. Turn right, horse stance, both fists punch
3. Step right, empty stance, hands guard
4. Right front kick
5. Step down into bow stance, right punch
6. Turn, drop stance to left
7. Rise up, single leg stand (crane)
8. Step down, salute, return to ready

*Repeat 3 times, moving with purpose!*

- 10 deep breaths
- Feel your body's energy

**Closing Salute**
- Fist and palm salute

- "Thank you for training together"

## Age Adaptations

### Kids (6-12)
- Focus on stances and kicks
- Make it a game: "Who can hold horse stance longest?"
- Movie connections: Kung Fu Panda, Mulan
- Animal style names: "Tiger punch! Crane kick!"
- Colored belt goals for motivation

### Teens
- Can handle longer stance holds
- Appreciate martial arts philosophy
- Add more complex techniques
- Watch and discuss martial arts films
- Consider local wushu classes

### Adults
- Full intensity practice
- Deep cultural exploration
- Stress-relieving focus required
- Model respect and discipline

### Modifications
- Higher stances for beginners
- Wall support for balance
- Focus on upper body if legs limited
- Chair wushu for seated practitioners
