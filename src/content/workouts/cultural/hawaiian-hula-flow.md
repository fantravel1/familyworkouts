## The Workout

### Opening: Honoring the Practice (2 minutes)

**Standing in Aloha**
- Feet together, hands at heart
- Deep breath in
- Arms open wide on exhale
- "We dance with aloha and respect"

**Hawaiian Sun Breath**
- Arms rise overhead (the sun rising)
- Circle arms down and wide (sun rays)
- Return to heart
- 4 rounds

### Basic Stance & Hip Work (5 minutes)

**Hula Stance**
- Feet hip-width apart
- Knees bent (stay bent throughout!)
- Pelvis tucked slightly
- Chest lifted, shoulders back
- Arms relaxed at sides
- "The lower you go, the better the workout!"

**Basic Kaholo (Side Step)**
1. Step RIGHT foot to right
2. Step LEFT foot to meet it
3. Step RIGHT again
4. Touch LEFT foot beside right
5. Reverse: Step LEFT to left...
- Continue side to side
- Arms flow naturally or rest on hips
- 2 minutes

** ªAmi (Hip Circles)**
- Stay in bent-knee stance
- Circle hips clockwise (like stirring a pot)
- 8 circles right
- 8 circles left
- Keep upper body still!
- "The hips tell the story of the ocean waves"

**Ka'o (Hip Sway)**
- Step right, lift left hip
- Step left, lift right hip
- Smooth sway, like a palm tree
- 16 counts

### Story Dance: "The Ocean Story" (8 minutes)

We'll dance a simple story. Follow along:

**Verse 1: The Rising Sun (2 min)**
"The sun rises over the mountain"
- Sun hands rising
- Mountain hands
- Kaholo side to side
- Face lights up with warmth

**Verse 2: The Rain Falls (2 min)**
"Rain falls gently on the flowers"
- Rain hands descending
- Flower hands opening
-  ªAmi hip circles
- Peaceful expression

**Verse 3: The Ocean Calls (2 min)**
"The ocean waves call us home"
- Wave arms
- Kaholo toward center (the ocean)
- Arms reach forward (answering the call)
- Longing expression

**Verse 4: Rainbow Promise (2 min)**
"The rainbow shows aloha for all"
- Rainbow arms
- Love hands to heart
- Open arms wide to family
- Joyful, grateful expression

### Free Flow: Dance the Story Your Way (3 minutes)

- Put on Hawaiian music
- Tell any story using your gestures
- Move your hips
- Let your face express emotion
- There's no wrong way to share aloha!

### Cool-Down: Mahalo (2 minutes)

**Gratitude Flow**
- Slow kaholo
- Hands at heart
- Gentle  ªami
- Breath work
- Final gesture: Hands at heart, small bow
- "Mahalo" (thank you)

## Age Adaptations

### Preschool & Young Kids (3-6)
- Focus on the stories‚Äîthey love storytelling
- Simpler gestures
- Shorter attention span: Pick 2-3 favorites
- Use stuffed animals or dolls dancing too
- Lots of aloha and encouragement!

### Kids (7-12)
- Can learn full gesture vocabulary
- Create their own stories
- Learn Hawaiian words
- Connect to geography lessons

### Teens
- Appreciate the depth and history
- Can handle longer sequences
- Connect to modern Hawaiian music and culture
- Social justice connections (Hawaiian sovereignty)

### Adults
- Full workout benefits
- Deep cultural appreciation
- Meditative qualities
- Great for stress relief

### Seniors
- Chair hula: All upper body gestures work
- Gentle standing with support
- Focus on hand movements
- Memory benefits from gesture learning
- Deeply meaningful practice
