## The Workout

### Opening Ritual (2 minutes)

**Rei (Bow)**
- Stand tall, feet together
- Arms at sides
- Bow from the waist
- "Onegaishimasu" (please teach me)

**Grounding Breath**
- Wide stance, knees bent
- Hands on thighs
- 4 deep breaths
- Feel your connection to the ground

### Warm-Up: Taiko Stretches (4 minutes)

**Arm Circles with Purpose**
- Big circles forward, visualize holding drumsticks
- Big circles backward
- 10 each direction
- Add a "HAH!" at the top of each circle

**Hip Rotations**
- Wide stance
- Circle hips like you're stirring a giant pot
- 10 each direction
- Taiko power comes from the hips!

**Taiko Squat**
- Wide sumo stance
- Drop into deep squat
- Arms reach forward then pull back
- 10 reps with "DON!" at the bottom

**Torso Twists**
- Wide stance, arms extended
- Twist right, left arm swings to right
- Twist left, right arm swings to left
- 20 total twists, building speed

### Main Workout: Drum Patterns (15 minutes)

**Pattern 1: The Big Drum (O-daiko)**
*Imaginary large drum in front of you*

1. Wide stance, knees bent
2. Arms raised high overhead
3. STRIKE down with both arms: "DON!"
4. Return to high position
5. 16 strikes

**Pattern 2: The Slant Drum (Naname)**
*Drum angled to your right*

1. Shift to right diagonal stance
2. Right arm high, left arm mid
3. Alternate strikes: Right "DON!" Left "DON!"
4. Full arm extension on each hit
5. 16 alternating strikes
6. Switch to left side, repeat

**Pattern 3: Running Beats (Oroshi)**
*Building intensity pattern*

1. Start with slow strikes
2. Gradually speed up
3. "DON...DON...DON..DON.DON.DONDONDONDO!"
4. Return to slow
5. Repeat 4 times

**Pattern 4: Horse Rhythm (Uma)**
*Classic taiko pattern*

1. "DON-doko-DON-doko-DON-doko-DON!"
2. Accent the "DON" beats
3. Full arm swing on DON
4. Smaller movements on "doko"
5. 8 full phrases

**Pattern 5: Power Strikes**
*Maximum intensity*

1. Wind up: arms sweep back behind you
2. Step forward into the strike
3. FULL POWER: "DON!!!"
4. Freeze at impact, full extension
5. Reset slowly
6. 8 strikes, alternating lead foot

**Pattern 6: Speed Challenge**
*As fast as you can with control*

1. Rapid alternating strikes
2. "Don-don-don-don-don-don-don-don"
3. 20 seconds ON
4. 10 seconds rest (freeze in position)
5. Repeat 4 rounds

### Combination: Full Taiko Piece (3 minutes)

Put it together:
- 8 counts: Big drum strikes
- 8 counts: Slant drum right
- 8 counts: Slant drum left
- 8 counts: Oroshi (building)
- 8 counts: Horse rhythm
- 8 counts: Power strikes
- FINALE: 16 fast strikes ending with one BIG STRIKE and freeze!

"SO-RE!"

### Cool-Down (2 minutes)

**Slow Strikes**
- Gentle, meditative striking
- Focus on breath
- Smooth movements
- 1 minute

**Closing Stretches**
- Arms overhead side stretch
- Shoulder rolls
- Deep forward fold
- Shake out arms and legs

**Closing Bow**
- Return to standing
- Bow: "Arigatou gozaimashita" (thank you very much)

## Family Taiko Games

### Rhythm Echo
- One person creates a 4-beat pattern
- Everyone echoes it back
- Patterns get more complex
- Can you stump your family?

### Speed Challenge
- Start at moderate tempo together
- Gradually increase speed
- Last one keeping up wins!

### Call and Response
- Leader: "DON DON!"
- Family: "DON DON!"
- Leader: "doko doko DON!"
- Family: "doko doko DON!"
- Create increasingly complex calls

### Freeze Taiko
- Drum along to music
- When music stops, FREEZE mid-strike
- Hold the position!
- Silliest freeze wins

## Cultural Deep Dive

### History of Taiko
- Drums used in Japan for 2000+ years
- Originally for communication, religious ceremonies, war
- Modern taiko ensemble (kumi-daiko) began in 1951
- Daihachi Oguchi created the first ensemble

### Types of Taiko Drums
- **O-daiko**: Giant drum (can be 6+ feet!)
- **Nagado-daiko**: Long-body drum (most common)
- **Shime-daiko**: High-pitched, rope-tuned drum
- **Okedo-daiko**: Barrel drum
