## A Safe Space to Move üíô

This workout is designed for individuals who benefit from predictable, calm, sensory-aware movement. There are no sudden changes, loud sounds, or unexpected elements. Everything follows a clear structure that you can learn and rely on.

## Who This Is For

- Children and adults with autism spectrum disorder
- People with sensory processing differences
- Those who feel overwhelmed by typical workout environments
- Anyone who prefers calm, predictable exercise
- Families with mixed sensory needs

## Design Principles

‚úì **Predictable structure**: Same format every time
‚úì **Visual supports**: Clear descriptions of each movement
‚úì **Calm environment**: No loud music required
‚úì **Proprioceptive input**: Grounding, "heavy work" movements
‚úì **Choice and control**: Options at every step
‚úì **No surprises**: You always know what comes next

## Before We Start

### Environment Setup
- Quiet space with minimal distractions
- Dim or natural lighting (avoid harsh fluorescents)
- Comfortable temperature
- Yoga mat or soft carpet
- Remove visual clutter if possible
- Timer visible (so you know how much longer)

### Optional Comfort Items
- Weighted blanket nearby (for cool-down)
- Fidget tool for waiting moments
- Noise-canceling headphones if needed
- Favorite stuffed animal or object

### The Schedule
We will do activities in this exact order:
1. Breathing (2 minutes)
2. Gentle stretching (4 minutes)
3. Standing movements (6 minutes)
4. Floor movements (5 minutes)
5. Rest time (3 minutes)

---

## Section 1: Breathing (2 minutes)

*You can sit or stand. You choose.*

### Balloon Breath
**We will do this 5 times.**

1. Breathe IN through your nose (count: 1, 2, 3, 4)
2. Your belly fills like a balloon
3. Breathe OUT through your mouth (count: 1, 2, 3, 4)
4. Your balloon deflates slowly
5. Repeat 4 more times

*This helps our bodies feel calm.*

### Hand Squeeze Breath
**We will do this 5 times.**

1. Breathe IN and squeeze your hands into fists (tight!)
2. Hold for a moment
3. Breathe OUT and open your hands wide (spread fingers!)
4. Feel the difference
5. Repeat 4 more times

*Squeezing and releasing helps our muscles relax.*

---

## Section 2: Gentle Stretching (4 minutes)

*All stretching is slow. Nothing fast.*

### Neck Stretches
**We will hold each position for 10 seconds.**

1. Look down (chin to chest) - 10 seconds
2. Look up (slowly) - 10 seconds
3. Tilt right ear to right shoulder - 10 seconds
4. Tilt left ear to left shoulder - 10 seconds
5. Return to center

*Slow and gentle. No rolling.*

### Arm Stretches
**We will do each stretch for 10 seconds.**

1. Reach both arms up high (10 seconds)
2. Reach right arm across body, left hand helps (10 seconds)
3. Reach left arm across body, right hand helps (10 seconds)
4. Arms down, shake them gently

### Side Stretches
**We will do each side for 10 seconds.**

1. Arms up, lean gently to the right (10 seconds)
2. Come back to center
3. Arms up, lean gently to the left (10 seconds)
4. Come back to center
5. Arms down

*You are doing great.*

---

## Section 3: Standing Movements (6 minutes)

*These are done standing. You may hold a wall if you want.*

### Marching (1 minute)
1. March in place
2. Lift knees gently (not high)
3. Arms swing naturally
4. We will march for 60 seconds
5. I will tell you when to stop

*March, march, march... nice and steady.*

### Wall Push-Ups (1 minute)
**We will do 10 wall push-ups.**

1. Stand facing a wall
2. Hands on wall, shoulder height
3. Lean toward wall
4. Push back
5. Repeat 9 more times
6. Rest

*Pushing gives your muscles good input.*

### Squats (1 minute)
**We will do 10 squats.**

1. Stand with feet apart
2. Sit back like sitting in a chair
3. Stand back up
4. Repeat 9 more times
5. Rest

*You can hold a chair for balance.*

### Arm Circles (1 minute)
**We will do this for 30 seconds each direction.**

1. Arms out to sides
2. Small circles forward (30 seconds)
3. Small circles backward (30 seconds)
4. Arms down

*Smooth circles. Not fast.*

### Heavy Walking (2 minutes)
1. Walk in a small circle or back and forth
2. Step with heavy feet (feel each step)
3. Stomp gently if it feels good
4. Arms swing naturally
5. We will walk for 2 minutes

*Heavy feet give your body good feedback.*

---

## Section 4: Floor Movements (5 minutes)

*Please move to your mat now. Lie on your back.*

### Leg Hugs (1 minute)
**We will do this 5 times.**

1. Pull right knee to chest
2. Hug it gently
3. Hold for 5 seconds
4. Release
5. Pull left knee to chest
6. Hug it gently
7. Hold for 5 seconds
8. Release
9. Repeat 4 more times

*Hugging your knees is calming.*

### Bridge (1 minute)
**We will do 8 bridges.**

1. Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat
2. Lift hips up
3. Hold for 3 seconds
4. Lower down
5. Repeat 7 more times

*Bridges make your body strong.*

### Turtle Pose (1 minute)
1. Turn over to hands and knees
2. Sit back on heels
3. Fold forward
4. Arms at your sides or stretched forward
5. Rest here for 1 minute
6. Breathe

*This is a hiding pose. Safe and quiet.*

### Gentle Rocking (2 minutes)
1. Lie on your back
2. Hug knees to chest
3. Rock gently side to side
4. Like a ball
5. Rock for 2 minutes
6. This is calming

*The rocking motion is soothing for your body.*

---

## Section 5: Rest Time (3 minutes)

### Final Rest
1. Lie on your back
2. Arms at your sides
3. Legs straight or bent (your choice)
4. Optional: Place a weighted blanket on your body
5. Close your eyes if comfortable (or soft gaze down)
6. We will rest for 3 minutes
7. I will tell you when it's time to finish

*You don't have to do anything. Just be.*

### Ending Transition
When rest is over:
1. Wiggle fingers and toes slowly
2. Take a deep breath
3. When you're ready, roll to one side
4. When you're ready, sit up slowly
5. The workout is complete

## Sensory Input Guide

### If You Need **More** Input:
- Heavier steps
- Stronger wall push-ups
- Squeeze muscles during movements
- Deep pressure (weighted blanket, tight hug)

### If You Need **LESS** Input:
- Gentler movements
- Skip anything uncomfortable
- Smaller range of motion
- More rest time

## For Parents and Caregivers

### Before the Workout
- Review the structure together
- Show the visual schedule
- Answer any questions
- Set up the environment calmly
- Allow comfort objects

### During the Workout
- Stay calm and quiet yourself
- Give simple verbal cues
- Don't touch without asking
- Allow processing time
- Praise effort, not performance

### Adapting for Individual Needs
- Skip exercises that cause distress
- Extend sections that are enjoyed
- Add more heavy work if needed
- Reduce transitions (combine sections)
- Allow movement breaks between sections

### Signs It's Going Well
- Regulated breathing
- Engagement (even if quiet)
- Completing movements
- Calm body language

### Signs to Adjust
- Covering ears
- Looking away repeatedly
- Increased stimming
- Verbal protest
- Physical withdrawal

*Always honor "no" or "stop" immediately.*
