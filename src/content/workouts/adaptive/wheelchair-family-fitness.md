# Wheelchair-Inclusive Family Fitness

Fitness has no barriers! This workout is designed so wheelchair users and ambulatory family members can exercise together, side-by-side.

## The Inclusive Philosophy

- **Everyone participates**: Modified versions for ALL
- **Same exercises, different positions**: Standing vs. seated options
- **Celebration of abilities**: Focus on what bodies CAN do
- **Together time**: Family fitness means everyone

## Before You Start

### Safety Considerations

**For Wheelchair Users:**
- Ensure brakes are locked during stationary exercises
- Clear space around wheelchair
- Have water within reach
- Stop if anything causes pain
- Know your individual limits

**For Ambulatory Family Members:**
- Be mindful of wheelchair space
- Don't grab wheelchair without permission
- Position yourself at eye level when possible
- Follow the seated person's pace

## Warm-Up: Wake Up Together (5 minutes)

### Upper Body Warm-Up

**Neck Stretches** (30 seconds each direction)
- *Wheelchair:* Gentle head turns, tilts
- *Standing:* Same movements

**Shoulder Rolls** (30 seconds)
- *Wheelchair:* Roll shoulders back and forward
- *Standing:* Same movements

**Arm Reaches** (1 minute)
- *Wheelchair:* Reach high, reach forward
- *Standing:* Same, can add toe raises

**Torso Twists** (1 minute)
- *Wheelchair:* Twist side to side
- *Standing:* Same, hips stay facing forward

**Wrist Circles** (30 seconds each direction)
- *Everyone:* Circle wrists both directions
- Important for wheelchair propulsion!

### Getting the Heart Rate Up (1.5 minutes)

**Arm Pumps**
- *Wheelchair:* Pump arms like running
- *Standing:* March in place while pumping arms
- 30 seconds

**Boxing Punches**
- *Wheelchair:* Jab-cross from seated position
- *Standing:* Same punches, can add bouncing
- 30 seconds

**Speed Arms**
- *Wheelchair:* Move arms as fast as possible
- *Standing:* Fast arms while speed walking in place
- 30 seconds

## Station 1: Cardio Burst (5 minutes)

### Exercise 1: Wheelchair Sprints / Running in Place

**Wheelchair User:**
1. Push forward vigorously (if space allows)
2. Or: Push wheel hand motions without moving
3. Go fast for 30 seconds!

**Standing Family:**
1. Run in place next to wheelchair
2. Or: Run circles around wheelchair
3. Match the energy!

**Duration:** 30 seconds ON, 15 seconds rest √ó 4 rounds

### Exercise 2: Battle Arms

**Everyone Together:**
1. Arms out to sides
2. Make fast small circles
3. Reverse direction
4. Speed up!
5. Feel the burn together!

**Duration:** 30 seconds ON, 15 seconds rest √ó 3 rounds

### Exercise 3: Upper Body Jacks

**Wheelchair User:**
1. Arms start at sides
2. Raise arms up and out (jumping jack motion)
3. Return to sides
4. Repeat fast!

**Standing Family:**
1. Full jumping jacks
2. Or: Seated position to match wheelchair user

**Duration:** 30 seconds ON, 15 seconds rest √ó 3 rounds

## Station 2: Strength Training (8 minutes)

### Exercise 1: Chest Press

**Wheelchair User:**
1. Elbows bent, fists at chest
2. Press forward
3. Bring back to chest
4. Add resistance band if available

**Standing Family:**
1. Same motion standing
2. Or: Wall push-ups between reps

**Reps:** 15, rest 30 seconds, repeat

### Exercise 2: Overhead Press

**Everyone:**
1. Hands at shoulders
2. Press up overhead
3. Lower with control
4. Keep core engaged

**Reps:** 12, rest 30 seconds, repeat

### Exercise 3: Rows (Air or Band)

**Wheelchair User:**
1. Arms extended forward
2. Pull elbows back (squeezing shoulder blades)
3. Extend forward again
4. Band adds resistance

**Standing Family:**
1. Same motion
2. Add slight forward lean
3. Or: Bent over row position

**Reps:** 15, rest 30 seconds, repeat

### Exercise 4: Bicep Curls

**Everyone:**
1. Arms at sides
2. Curl hands to shoulders
3. Lower with control
4. Use weights, bands, or just arms

**Reps:** 12 each arm (or both together)

### Exercise 5: Tricep Extensions

**Wheelchair User:**
1. Arms overhead, elbows bent
2. Straighten arms up
3. Lower back to start
4. Keep elbows pointing up

**Standing Family:**
1. Same motion
2. Can do one arm at a time

**Reps:** 12, rest 30 seconds, repeat

## Station 3: Core Work (5 minutes)

### Exercise 1: Seated Crunches

**Wheelchair User:**
1. Sit tall, hands on chest
2. Curl forward, bringing chest toward lap
3. Return to upright
4. Control the movement

**Standing Family:**
1. Same motion seated on chair
2. Or: Standing oblique crunches

**Reps:** 15-20

### Exercise 2: Twisting Reaches

**Everyone:**
1. Arms extended forward
2. Twist right, reach right
3. Return to center
4. Twist left, reach left
5. Engage obliques!

**Reps:** 10 each side

### Exercise 3: Seated Leg Extensions (if possible)

**Wheelchair User:**
1. Extend one leg forward (if able)
2. Hold 5 seconds
3. Lower with control
4. Switch legs
5. Or: Engage leg muscles without movement

**Standing Family:**
1. Stand on one leg
2. Extend other leg forward
3. Hold, switch

**Reps:** 8 each leg

### Exercise 4: Arm-Core Connection

**Everyone:**
1. Arms out to sides
2. Bring right elbow to left knee (or knee area)
3. Return to start
4. Bring left elbow to right knee
5. Keep core tight!

**Reps:** 10 each side

## Station 4: Family Partner Exercises (5 minutes)

### Exercise 1: Partner Claps

**Face Each Other:**
1. High-five right hands
2. High-five left hands
3. Both hands together
4. Get faster!
5. Add patterns (pat-a-cake style)

**Duration:** 1 minute

### Exercise 2: Resistance Push

**How To:**
1. Place palms against each other
2. Push against partner's resistance
3. Both people engage muscles
4. Take turns "winning" the push

**Duration:** 30 seconds √ó 3 rounds

### Exercise 3: Mirror Movement

**How To:**
1. One person leads
2. Other person mirrors exactly
3. Leader does upper body movements
4. Follower copies in real-time
5. Switch leaders!

**Duration:** 1 minute each leader

### Exercise 4: Pass the Ball (or Object)

**How To:**
1. Use soft ball, balloon, or pillow
2. Pass back and forth
3. Change heights (high, low, side)
4. Speed up!
5. Don't let it drop!

**Duration:** 2 minutes

## Station 5: Fun Games (5 minutes)

### Game 1: Wheelchair Dance Party!

**How To:**
1. Play upbeat music
2. Wheelchair user does seated dance
3. Ambulatory family does standing dance
4. Everyone grooves together!
5. Copy each other's moves!

**Duration:** 2 minutes

### Game 2: Simon Says (Seated Edition)

**How To:**
1. Take turns being Simon
2. Only upper body/seated movements
3. "Simon says raise your right arm!"
4. "Simon says make a funny face!"
5. Everyone plays together!

**Duration:** 2 minutes

### Game 3: Balloon Volleyball

**How To:**
1. Blow up a balloon
2. Tap back and forth
3. Don't let it touch the ground!
4. Count your rallies
5. Beat your record!

**Duration:** 3 minutes (or as long as fun!)

## Cool-Down: Relax Together (5 minutes)

### Gentle Stretches

**Neck Stretch** (30 seconds each side)
- Tilt head toward shoulder
- Gentle hold, no pulling
- Both sides

**Shoulder Stretch** (30 seconds each)
- Arm across body
- Other hand supports at elbow
- Gentle stretch

**Tricep Stretch** (30 seconds each)
- Arm overhead, elbow bent
- Other hand gently presses elbow
- Stretch back of arm

**Side Stretch** (30 seconds each side)
- One arm reaches up and over
- Lean toward opposite side
- Feel ribs open

**Forward Fold** (1 minute)
- *Wheelchair:* Lean forward in chair
- *Standing:* Fold at hips
- Relax head and arms

### Breathing Together (1 minute)

1. Close eyes (if comfortable)
2. Inhale for 4 counts
3. Hold for 4 counts
4. Exhale for 4 counts
5. Hold for 4 counts
6. Repeat 4 times

### Gratitude Moment (1 minute)

1. Each person shares one thing
2. "I'm grateful for this time together"
3. "I'm grateful my body can do _____"
4. End with a group hand hold

## Modifications & Alternatives

### For Different Ability Levels

**Limited Arm Mobility:**
- Smaller range of motion
- Use only one arm
- Focus on what feels good
- Assistive stretching with partner

**Higher Mobility:**
- Add resistance bands
- Increase speed
- Add weights if available
- More repetitions

### Equipment Options

**No Equipment:**
- All exercises work with just body

**With Resistance Bands:**
- Attach to wheelchair for rows
- Hold for bicep curls
- Use for chest press

**With Light Weights:**
- 1-5 pounds adds challenge
- Water bottles work too!
- Canned goods in a pinch
