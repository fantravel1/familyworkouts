# Senior Gentle Movement (Ages 65+)

Movement matters at every age! This workout is designed with the needs of older adults in mind‚Äîfocusing on balance, flexibility, strength, and overall wellbeing.

## Why Movement Matters for Seniors

- **Fall prevention**: Balance exercises reduce fall risk by 23%
- **Independence**: Strength maintains ability to do daily tasks
- **Brain health**: Exercise improves cognitive function
- **Mood boost**: Movement reduces depression and anxiety
- **Social connection**: Family workouts combat isolation
- **Pain management**: Gentle movement reduces joint stiffness

## Before You Begin

### Safety Checklist

‚úÖ Cleared by doctor for exercise
‚úÖ Sturdy chair without wheels nearby
‚úÖ Clear space, no trip hazards
‚úÖ Non-slip footwear or bare feet
‚úÖ Water within reach
‚úÖ Family member aware/present

### Listen to Your Body

- Pain means STOP
- Dizziness means SIT
- Shortness of breath means PAUSE
- Some muscle fatigue is normal
- Sharp pain is NOT normal

## Equipment Needed

- Sturdy chair with no arms (or dining chair)
- Optional: Light hand weights (1-3 lbs) or water bottles
- Wall nearby for balance support

## Warm-Up: Gentle Awakening (4 minutes)

### Seated Start

**Seated March** (1 minute)
- Sit tall in chair
- Lift one knee, then the other
- Gentle marching motion
- Arms can swing naturally
- Nice and easy!

**Ankle Circles** (30 seconds each foot)
- Extend one leg
- Circle ankle clockwise
- Circle counterclockwise
- Switch feet
- Wakes up the joints!

**Shoulder Rolls** (30 seconds)
- Roll shoulders forward
- Roll shoulders backward
- Big, slow circles
- Release tension

**Neck Stretches** (30 seconds each side)
- Gently tilt ear to shoulder
- Hold 15 seconds
- Other side
- Never force!

**Arm Reaches** (1 minute)
- Reach up toward ceiling
- Reach forward
- Reach to each side
- Gentle, flowing movements

## Standing Balance (4 minutes)

*Use chair back for support as needed*

### Balance Series

**Heel-Toe Stand** (1 minute)
- Stand with one foot directly in front of other
- Heel touching toe
- Hold 15 seconds
- Switch feet
- Chair support if needed!

**Single Leg Stand** (1 minute)
- Hold chair back
- Lift one foot slightly off floor
- Hold 15 seconds
- Switch legs
- Work up to hands-free!

**Heel Raises** (1 minute)
- Hold chair back
- Rise up on tiptoes
- Lower slowly
- 10-15 repetitions
- Builds calf strength for walking!

**Weight Shifts** (1 minute)
- Feet hip-width apart
- Shift weight to right foot
- Hold 5 seconds
- Shift to left foot
- Gentle rocking motion

## Seated Strength (6 minutes)

*All exercises performed in chair*

### Upper Body

**Seated Arm Raises** (1 minute)
- Sit tall
- Arms at sides
- Raise arms forward to shoulder height
- Lower slowly
- 10-12 repetitions
- Add light weights to progress

**Bicep Curls** (1 minute)
- Arms at sides, palms up
- Bend elbows, hands toward shoulders
- Lower slowly
- 10-12 repetitions
- Use weights if comfortable

**Overhead Press** (1 minute)
- Hands at shoulders
- Press up toward ceiling
- Lower to shoulders
- 10-12 repetitions
- Great for reaching high shelves!

### Lower Body

**Seated Leg Extensions** (1 minute)
- Sit tall, feet flat
- Extend one leg straight
- Hold 3 seconds
- Lower slowly
- 8 each leg
- Strengthens knees!

**Seated Marching** (1 minute)
- Lift knees alternating
- Higher than warm-up pace
- Arms pumping
- 30 seconds moderate effort
- Great for hip flexors!

**Seated Heel-Toe Raises** (1 minute)
- Feet flat on floor
- Lift heels (toes stay down)
- Lower heels, lift toes
- Rock back and forth
- 15 complete cycles
- Ankle strength for walking!

## Standing Strength (4 minutes)

*Chair nearby for support*

### Functional Movements

**Chair Stand** (1.5 minutes)
- Sit at edge of chair
- Feet flat, slightly back
- Stand up without using hands (if possible)
- Sit back down slowly
- 8-10 repetitions
- USE hands if needed‚Äîthat's okay!

**Wall Push-Ups** (1.5 minutes)
- Face wall, arm's length away
- Hands on wall, shoulder height
- Lean in (bend elbows)
- Push back out
- 10-15 repetitions
- Adjust distance for difficulty

**Supported Squats** (1 minute)
- Hold chair back
- Feet hip-width apart
- Slight squat (as comfortable)
- Stand back up
- 8-10 repetitions
- Only go as low as comfortable!

## Flexibility & Stretching (4 minutes)

### Seated Stretches

**Overhead Stretch** (30 seconds)
- Interlace fingers
- Reach toward ceiling
- Gentle stretch
- Breathe deeply

**Seated Twist** (30 seconds each side)
- Sit tall
- Cross arms over chest
- Twist right, hold
- Twist left, hold
- Spine mobility!

**Hamstring Stretch** (30 seconds each leg)
- Extend one leg
- Gently lean forward
- Feel stretch in back of thigh
- Don't bounce!
- Switch legs

**Figure Four Stretch** (30 seconds each side)
- Sit tall
- Cross ankle over opposite knee
- Gentle lean forward
- Feel hip stretch
- Switch sides

**Seated Cat-Cow** (1 minute)
- Hands on thighs
- Round back (cat)
- Arch back gently (cow)
- Flow with breath
- Spinal mobility!

## Cool-Down: Breathing & Relaxation (2 minutes)

### Calming Finish

**Deep Breathing** (1 minute)
- Close eyes if comfortable
- Breathe in through nose (4 counts)
- Hold briefly (2 counts)
- Exhale through mouth (6 counts)
- 5 deep breaths

**Gratitude Moment** (30 seconds)
- Think of one thing you're grateful for
- One thing your body can do
- Appreciate this moment

**Final Stretch** (30 seconds)
- Gentle reach up
- Gentle reach forward
- Roll shoulders
- "I moved my body today!"

## Modifications

### If Balance Is Challenging
- Keep one hand on chair at all times
- Sit for more exercises
- Focus on seated balance work
- Always have support nearby

### If Joints Are Stiff
- Smaller range of motion
- More warm-up time
- Gentle movements only
- Heat pad before exercise can help

### If Using a Walker/Cane
- Walker provides stable support
- Standing exercises with walker
- Seated exercises without
- Don't sacrifice safety for exercise

### After Surgery/Injury
- Get doctor clearance first
- Physical therapist exercises priority
- Very gentle movements only
- Listen to body carefully

## Benefits for Specific Conditions

### Arthritis
- Movement lubricates joints
- Reduces stiffness
- Maintains range of motion
- Start gentle, build slowly

### Osteoporosis
- Weight-bearing exercises help bones
- Balance prevents falls
- Strength protects from fractures
- Avoid high-impact movements

### Heart Conditions
- Gentle cardio improves circulation
- Don't overexert
- Monitor breathing
- Doctor-approved intensity

### Diabetes
- Exercise helps blood sugar control
- Monitor levels
- Have snack available
- Regular movement matters
