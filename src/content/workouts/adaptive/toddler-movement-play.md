# Toddler Movement & Play (Ages 2-4)

Movement for the smallest movers! These activities are designed specifically for toddler attention spans, motor development, and the joy of moving with a caring adult.

## Understanding Toddler Movement

### What to Expect

**Age 2:**
- Can walk, run, and climb
- Kicks a ball forward
- Stands on tiptoes
- May pedal a tricycle
- Attention span: 2-5 minutes

**Age 3:**
- Runs easily
- Climbs well
- Pedals a tricycle
- Hops and stands on one foot
- Attention span: 3-8 minutes

**Age 4:**
- Hops on one foot
- Catches a bounced ball
- May skip
- Moves forward and backward
- Attention span: 5-10 minutes

### Key Principles

1. **Play-based**: It's ALL play, not "exercise"
2. **Adult-led**: Parent participates fully
3. **Short bursts**: Follow their attention
4. **Repetition loved**: They WANT to do it again
5. **Praise liberally**: Celebrate all attempts
6. **Safety first**: Clear space, supervision always

## Activity 2: Follow the Leader (2 minutes)

### Copy Me!

Parent leads, toddler follows:

**Movement Ideas:**
- Clap hands (up high, down low)
- Stomp feet
- Touch toes
- Reach for the sky
- Spin in a circle
- Pat your head
- Wave goodbye
- Jump up and down
- March in place
- Wiggle all over!

**Pro tip:** Go SLOWLY and repeat each action 3-4 times.

**Role switch:** Let toddler be the leader sometimes! (Hilarious results guaranteed)

**More body parts:**

## Activity 4: Freeze Dance! (3 minutes)

### Dance and Stop!

**How to play:**
1. Play music (any happy song)
2. Dance together!
3. Stop the music
4. FREEZE! Stay very still!
5. Start music, dance again!

**Why toddlers love it:**
- Predictable pattern
- Permission to be silly
- Control element (they decide their pose)
- Music + movement = joy

**Tip:** Exaggerate YOUR freeze and make funny faces

## Activity 5: Balloon Fun (3 minutes)

*Requires one balloon*

### Balloon Play Ideas

**Keep It Up:**
- Tap balloon to keep it in air
- Count taps together
- "Don't let it touch the ground!"

**Balloon Volleyball:**
- Gently tap back and forth
- No net needed
- Just tapping fun!

**Chase the Balloon:**
- Let it float down
- Run to catch it
- Throw it up again

**Body Parts Tap:**
- "Can you tap it with your hand?"
- "Can you tap it with your head?"
- "Can you tap it with your knee?"

## Activity 6: Tunnel and Bridge (2 minutes)

### Parent as Equipment!

**Tunnel:**
- Parent on hands and knees
- Toddler crawls under
- "Go through the tunnel!"
- Repeat, repeat, repeat!

**Bridge:**
- Parent in crab position (belly up)
- Toddler crawls under
- "Go under the bridge!"
- Watch for tickle hands!

**Mountain:**
- Parent lies down
- Toddler climbs over
- "Climb the mountain!"
- Gentle rolling encouraged

## Activity 7: Red Light, Green Light - Toddler Style

### Simple Stop and Go

**How to play:**
- "Green light!" = Walk toward parent
- "Red light!" = Stop!
- Big hugs when they reach you!

**Variations:**
- Yellow light = slow motion
- Blue light = tip toes
- Purple light = bunny hops

**Key:** Be VERY excited when they stop on red!

## Activity 8: Parachute Play

*Use a bedsheet as a parachute*

### Sheet Fun!

**Waves:**
- Each hold an edge
- Make gentle waves
- Make BIG waves!
- Ocean sounds!

**Mushroom:**
- Lift sheet up high
- Run underneath
- Sheet floats down
- Magic mushroom!

**Ball Roll:**
- Put soft ball on sheet
- Try to roll it around
- Don't let it fall off!
- Teamwork!

## Activity 9: Obstacle Course (3 minutes)

### Living Room Adventure!

**Simple Setup:**
1. Pillow to climb over
2. Tunnel (blanket over chairs)
3. Line to walk on (tape)
4. Soft target to throw ball at
5. Finish line (high five!)

**Tips:**
- Keep it simple (3-5 obstacles max)
- Demonstrate first
- Help when needed
- Celebrate completion!
- Reset and go again!

## Activity 10: Calm Down Cuddles (2 minutes)

### Ending with Connection

**Gentle Wind Down:**

*Sit together*

"Let's take a big breath in..." (breathe together)
"And blow it out like birthday candles..." (exhale)

"Let's make ourselves into a little ball..." (curl up)
"Now stretch out like a big star!" (spread out)

"Time for quiet snuggles..." (hug)
"Great job moving your body today!"

- Finger wiggles
- Nose touches
- Shoulder shrugs
- Seated marching

### Before Meal Movement

Get wiggles out:

## Parent Tips

### For Success

1. **Get on their level** - Floor time together
2. **Be enthusiastic** - Your energy = their energy
3. **Expect chaos** - It won't look like a "workout"
4. **Short is fine** - 5 minutes is great!
5. **Repeat favorites** - They love doing things again
6. **Silly is best** - Laughter is cardio
7. **End before meltdown** - Quit while ahead!

### Safety Notes

- Clear the space of hazards
- Stay within arm's reach
- Bare feet or non-slip socks
- Watch for fatigue signs
- Hydration breaks as needed

### Developmental Benefits

**Physical:**
- Gross motor skill development
- Balance and coordination
- Body awareness
- Strength building

**Cognitive:**
- Following directions
- Sequencing
- Cause and effect
- Language development

**Social-Emotional:**
- Parent-child bonding
- Self-confidence
- Positive movement associations
- Emotional regulation through movement
