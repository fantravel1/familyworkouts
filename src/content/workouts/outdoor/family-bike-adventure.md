## Safety Rules for the Road

### The Family Biking Code

1. **Helmets ON before wheels roll**
2. **Ride in single file on roads**
3. **Adults front AND back** (kids in middle)
4. **Obey traffic signals and signs**
5. **Use hand signals**
6. **Call out hazards** ("Pothole!" "Car back!")
7. **Stay together‚Äîno one left behind**

### Hand Signals (Teach Everyone!)

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Left arm straight out | Turning left |
| Left arm up, bent at elbow | Turning right |
| Left arm down, palm back | Stopping |
| Pointing down | Hazard on ground |

### Road Rules for Kids

- Stay on the right side
- Never ride into traffic
- Stop and look at every intersection
- Walk bikes across busy streets
- Stay on sidewalks when very young

## Ride Types by Family Stage

### üçº Families with Babies/Toddlers

**Options:**
- Baby seat (rear-mounted after 9 months)
- Bike trailer (6 months+, reclined)
- Cargo bike with kid compartment

**Tips:**
- Short distances (15-30 min)
- Smooth paths only
- Extra padding for bumps
- Frequent stops
- Watch for sleep/discomfort

### üõ¥ Families with Preschoolers (3-5)

- 20-30 minute rides max
- Stop at playgrounds!
- Bring snacks
- Celebrate small victories

### üö≤ Families with Kids (6-10)

- Involve them in route planning

### üöµ Families with Tweens/Teens (11+)

## Games to Play While Riding

### I Spy (On Wheels)
- "I spy something... BLUE!"
- Spotter can't reveal until it's passed
- Everyone guesses while riding

### Alphabet Hunt
- Find things starting with each letter
- A = Acorn, B = Bench, C = Car...
- First to Z wins!

### Color Quest
- Before riding, pick a color
- Count how many things you see in that color
- Compare at the end

### Animal Safari
- Spot and identify animals
- Points: Dog = 1, Bird = 1, Squirrel = 2, Rabbit = 3, Deer = 5
- Keep a running tally

### Story Relay
- One person starts a story (3 sentences)
- Next person continues
- Create an adventure while having one!

### Speed Challenges (Safe Areas Only!)
- Race to the next landmark
- Slow-motion race (last one wins!)
- Who can coast the longest?

## Trail Etiquette

### On Multi-Use Paths

**Yield to:**
- Pedestrians (always)
- Horses (if on equestrian trails)

**Pass safely:**
- Call out "On your left!" before passing
- Pass with space
- Slow down near pedestrians

**Be courteous:**
- Don't block the path when stopped
- Control your speed
- Smile and wave!

### On Mountain Bike Trails

**Yield to:**
- Hikers
- Uphill traffic (they're working harder!)

**Trail care:**
- Stay on marked trails
- Don't skid (damages trail)
- Pack out trash

## Weather Considerations

### üåû Hot Weather
- Start early morning or evening
- Bring LOTS of water
- Apply sunscreen
- Seek shaded routes
- Take more breaks
- Watch for overheating signs

### üåßÔ∏è Wet Weather
- Generally avoid (slippery!)
- If caught: slow down significantly
- Brake earlier and gentler
- Avoid painted lines (slippery)
- Dry bikes after

### üí® Windy Days
- Plan route to return with tailwind
- Lower riding position
- Smaller riders struggle more
- Adjust expectations

### ‚ùÑÔ∏è Cold Weather
- Layer up (warm, but not restrictive)
- Protect extremities (fingers, ears)
- Watch for ice
- Shorter rides

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### "I'm tired!"
- Take a break (no shame!)
- Snack break boosts energy
- Shorter distance next time
- Consider trail-a-bike for backup

### "My legs hurt!"
- Check bike fit (seat height)
- Easier gears
- Slower pace
- Build up gradually

### "I'm scared!"
- Walk tricky sections
- Practice in parking lots
- Build confidence slowly
- Stay close together

### "It's boring!"
- Add games
- Have a destination
- Bring a friend
- Choose more interesting routes
