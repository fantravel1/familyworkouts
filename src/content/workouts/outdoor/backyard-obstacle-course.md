## Build Your Family Adventure!

Transform your backyard (or park!) into an epic obstacle course using items you already have. This workout is as much about creativity and teamwork as it is about fitness.

## Why Obstacle Courses Rock

- **Full-body workout**: Running, jumping, climbing, crawling
- **Problem-solving**: Navigate challenges
- **Teamwork**: Build it together, conquer it together
- **Adaptable**: Easy to adjust difficulty
- **Repeatable**: Change it up every time!

## What You'll Need

**Use what you have! Substitutions welcome:**

- Cones or water bottles (markers)
- Jump rope or pool noodles
- Hula hoops or chalk circles
- Buckets or boxes
- Towels or mats
- Chairs or stools
- Balls of various sizes
- Timer or phone

**Pro tip**: Let kids help gather and choose items!

---

## The Ultimate Family Course

### Station 1: Starting Sprint
**Distance**: 20 feet / 6 meters

- Mark start and first station
- Sprint or run as fast as you can
- Modification: Fast walk or skip

### Station 2: Cone Weave
**Items**: 5 cones (or water bottles) in a line

- Weave in and out through cones
- Touch each cone as you pass
- Modification: Walk instead of run

### Station 3: Jump Zone
**Items**: 3-4 hula hoops (or chalk circles)

- Jump from circle to circle
- Two feet or one foot
- Don't touch the "lava" outside circles!
- Modification: Step instead of jump

### Station 4: Tunnel Crawl
**Items**: Table, sheet, or pool noodle arch

- Army crawl or bear crawl through
- Stay low!
- Modification: Walk under instead

### Station 5: Balance Beam
**Items**: 2x4 board, tape line, or jump rope on ground

- Walk length without stepping off
- Arms out for balance
- Modification: Walk alongside the line

### Station 6: Ball Toss
**Items**: Bucket + 3 balls (any type)

- Stand at marked distance
- Make all 3 balls in bucket before moving on
- Modification: Stand closer or larger target

### Station 7: Agility Ladder
**Items**: Jump ropes laid in ladder pattern (or chalk)

- Quick feet through each box
- Different patterns: 2 feet, 1 foot, sideways
- Modification: Slow and steady steps

### Station 8: Over-Under
**Items**: Pool noodles held by family members or on chairs

- Jump over low ones
- Crawl under high ones
- Alternate through 4-6 obstacles
- Modification: Step over/duck under

### Station 9: Strength Challenge
**Pick one or all:**

- 10 jumping jacks
- 5 push-ups
- 10 squats
- 30-second plank

### Station 10: Victory Sprint!
**Distance**: 20 feet to finish line

- Give it everything you've got!
- Cross the line like a champion!
- Celebrate!!!

## How to Play

### Option 1: Time Trial
- One person at a time
- Time each run
- Try to beat your own record
- Everyone cheers the runner!

### Option 2: Relay Race
- Divide into teams
- Tag team member when you finish
- First team with all members done wins
- Make teams fair (mix ages/abilities)

### Option 3: Together Mode
- Whole family does course together
- Stay together, help each other
- Not a race‚Äîan adventure!

### Option 4: Creator's Choice
- Each family member designs one station
- Rotate who adds to the course
- Complete everyone's creation

## Age Adaptations

### Preschool (3-5)
- Shorter course (5-6 stations)
- Simpler tasks
- Parent runs alongside
- Lots of celebration!
- Example mods:
  - Walk the balance beam holding hands
  - Roll balls toward bucket
  - Step through hoops

### Kids (6-12)
- Full course is perfect
- Add timing for extra motivation
- Let them modify/add stations
- Competition is okay here
- Challenge: Complete course twice!

### Teens
- Make it harder!
- Add burpees or bear crawls
- Increase distances
- Time pressure
- Let them design family challenges

### Adults
- Go full speed‚Äîmodel effort
- Add strength stations
- Compete against yourself
- Great cardio workout!

### Seniors
- Walking-pace modifications
- Skip crawling stations
- Focus on balance and agility stations
- Important: Choose safe surfaces

## Building Tips

1. **Flow**: Stations should flow naturally, no backtracking
2. **Variety**: Mix running, jumping, crawling, throwing
3. **Safety**: Check for hazards, secure loose items
4. **Rest**: Natural rest happens between hard stations
5. **Spectating**: Design so waiting family can watch/cheer

---

## Progression Ideas

**Week 1**: Basic 6-station course
**Week 2**: Add 2 more stations
**Week 3**: Add timing, beat your record
**Week 4**: Add strength challenges
**Week 5**: Full 10-station competitive course!

## Safety Reminders

- Check all equipment is secure
- Remove hazards from course area
- Proper footwear (or safe bare feet)
- Supervise younger children
- Hydrate between runs
- Know when to rest
